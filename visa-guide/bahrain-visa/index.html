Required Documents

Every visa application requires proof of identity, purpose of visit, and financial capacity.

Core Documents (All Visa Types)

  • Valid Indian passport — Must have at least 6 months validity beyond intended stay. At least 2 blank pages.
  • Digital photograph — 35×45mm, white background, taken within 6 months.
  • Completed visa application form
  • Previous visas and travel history — Include copies of previous Schengen, US, UK visas and travel stamps.
  • Cover letter — Explaining purpose of visit, duration, itinerary, and ties to India.

Travel Insurance Requirements

Travel insurance is recommended for all visitors to Bahrain:

  • Tourist/Business Visitors: Comprehensive travel insurance covering medical emergencies, trip cancellation, and baggage loss is strongly recommended.
  • Students: Health insurance may be mandatory for student visa holders.
  • Work Visa Holders: Employer-provided health coverage is typically required.

Best Time to Visit Bahrain

Bahrain has a hot desert climate with very mild winters and extremely hot summers. Timing your visit is crucial for comfort:

  • Best Season (November to March): The most pleasant period with temperatures of 18-25°C. Perfect for exploring Manama's souks, the Bahrain Fort, and outdoor attractions. The Bahrain Grand Prix typically takes place in March.
  • Spring (April to May): Temperatures rise to 28-38°C. Still manageable for sightseeing if you plan activities for early morning or evening. Fewer tourists than winter.
  • Summer (June to September): Extremely hot with temperatures exceeding 45°C. High humidity along the coast. Indoor activities like shopping malls and water parks are popular. Hotel rates are at their lowest.
  • Autumn (October): Temperatures start to cool down to 30-35°C. A transitional month with improving conditions for outdoor activities.

Common Reasons for Bahrain Visa Rejection

Bahrain's visa system is generally efficient, but rejections can occur for these reasons:

  • Sponsorship Issues: Bahrain requires a local sponsor (hotel, company, or individual) for most visa types. Not having a valid sponsor registered with the authorities is a common issue.
  • Incorrect eVisa Category: Applying for the wrong visa type — tourist vs business vs visit — results in immediate rejection. Check eligibility on the Bahrain eVisa portal before applying.
  • Passport Expiry: Passport must be valid for at least 6 months from the date of arrival. eVisa applications with passports expiring sooner are automatically rejected.
  • Criminal Background: Bahrain conducts background checks. Any criminal record, especially related to drugs or financial crimes, leads to visa denial.
  • Previous Visa Violations: Overstaying a previous Bahrain visa or working on a tourist visa can result in a ban and future visa rejections.

Travel Tips for Bahrain

Navigate the Kingdom of Bahrain with these essential tips:

  • Currency: Bahraini Dinar (BHD) is one of the strongest currencies in the world (1 BHD ≈ 2.65 USD). Credit cards are widely accepted. ATMs are abundant in Manama.
  • Transportation: Bahrain International Airport (BAH) is located on Muharraq Island, connected to Manama by the Sheikh Isa Causeway. Taxis and ride-hailing apps like Uber and Careem are available. Public buses connect major areas but are less frequent.
  • Language: Arabic is the official language. English is widely spoken, especially in business, hospitality, and shopping areas. Hindi and Urdu are also common due to the large expatriate community.
  • Dress Code: Bahrain is more liberal than other Gulf states but modest dress is expected in public. Shorts and sleeveless tops are acceptable in malls and hotels but not in government buildings or traditional areas. Swimwear is for beaches and pools only.
  • Cuisine: Try Machboos (spiced rice with meat), Muhammar (sweet rice with fish), and fresh seafood. Manama's Bab Al Bahrain area has excellent traditional restaurants. Alcohol is available in licensed hotels and restaurants.
  • Attractions: Don't miss the Bahrain National Museum, Qal'at al-Bahrain (Bahrain Fort, UNESCO site), the Tree of Life, and the Al-Fateh Grand Mosque. The Bahrain International Circuit hosts the annual Formula 1 Grand Prix.
