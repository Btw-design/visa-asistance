China 240-Hour Visa-Free Transit 2026 – Complete Guide

Published: July 10, 2026 10 min read By Ajit Mane

China has significantly expanded its visa-free transit policy, now allowing eligible travellers to stay for up to 240 hours (10 days) without applying for a visa in advance. This China 10 day visa free transit scheme is the most generous visa-free offering China has ever implemented, enabling a China transit without visa for eligible nationals through 60 designated ports. This 240-hour visa-free transit China policy, effective since December 2024, replaces the earlier 72-hour and 144-hour schemes and now covers 55 eligible countries, 60 designated ports, and 24 provinces.

For non-eligible nationals, including Indian passport holders, a standard China visa remains mandatory. This guide covers everything you need to know about the China 240 hour visa free transit policy, including eligible countries for China visa free transit 2026, entry ports, permitted provinces, the "third country" rule, step-by-step application process, and most importantly, what Indian travellers need to do instead.

Key Takeaways

  • 240 hours (10 days) visa-free stay for eligible passport holders transiting through China
  • 55 countries eligible — India is NOT on the list
  • 60 designated ports including airports, seaports, and land borders
  • 24 provinces open for travel; 7 provinces excluded
  • Must hold a confirmed onward ticket to a third country or region
  • Indian passport holders need a China Tourist (L) Visa — applied via COVA + CVASC

What Is the China 240-Hour Visa-Free Transit Policy?

The China 240 hour visa free transit policy is a government scheme that allows citizens of 55 eligible countries to enter China without a visa for up to 240 hours (10 days), provided they are transiting to a third country or region. The policy was expanded from the earlier 144-hour (6-day) scheme in December 2024 by the National Immigration Administration (NIA) of China.

Key changes under the expanded policy include:

  • Stay period extended from 72/144 hours to 240 hours (10 days)
  • Designated ports increased from 39 to 60
  • Open provinces expanded from 19 to 24 (added: Shanxi, Anhui, Jiangxi, Hainan, Guizhou)
  • Cross-province travel now permitted within the 24 designated regions
  • New port types include the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge and West Kowloon Express Rail Station

During the visa-free transit period, travellers can engage in tourism, business visits, exchange visits, and family visits. However, the policy strictly does not cover employment, study, or journalistic activities, which require appropriate visas.

Important for Indian Readers

Indian passport holders are NOT eligible for the 240-hour visa-free transit policy. Citizens of India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Vietnam, the Philippines, and most African countries cannot use this scheme and must apply for a standard China visa through the COVA online portal and CVASC centres.

If you hold a passport from any of the 55 countries listed above, you can enter China visa-free for up to 10 days when transiting to a third destination. The full list was most recently updated in June 2025 when Indonesia was added as the 55th country.

24 Open Provinces and Excluded Regions

The 240 hour transit China scheme allows travel within 24 designated provincial-level regions. Seven regions are excluded and require a separate visa or permit:

Travellers under the China 240 hour visa free transit can freely move between different provinces within the 24 open regions. For example, you could fly into Beijing, take the high-speed train to Shanghai, visit Hangzhou, and depart from Shanghai, all within the 240-hour window. However, you cannot enter any of the 7 excluded regions without additional permits.

Newly Added Provinces

The 2024 expansion added 5 new provinces: Shanxi (home to the "Black Myth: Wukong" filming locations), Anhui (entire province open), Jiangxi (Nanchang and Jingdezhen), Hainan (entire province open, including the tropical island), and Guizhou (entire province open, famous for world natural heritage sites).

Third Country Rule – Critical Requirement

The single most important rule of the China visa-free transit policy is the "third country" requirement. Your itinerary must be: Country A → China → Country B, where Country A and Country B are different.

This means:

  • A round-trip ticket from your home country to China and back does NOT qualify (Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan count as separate regions)
  • Example of valid itinerary: London (UK) → Beijing (China) → Bangkok (Thailand)
  • Example of invalid itinerary: London (UK) → Beijing (China) → London (UK)
  • Your onward flight must depart within 240 hours of your arrival in China

Requirements and Documents

To use the 240 hour visa free transit China scheme, you must present the following at the port of entry:

Required Documents

  • Valid ordinary passport from one of the 55 eligible countries (minimum 3 months validity beyond entry date, with at least 2 blank visa pages)
  • Confirmed onward ticket to a third country or region (must have a confirmed seat and PNR, departing within 240 hours)
  • Completed China Digital Arrival Card (free online form, QR code received on registration)
  • Hotel booking confirmation for your stay (recommended, not mandatory)
  • Travel insurance certificate (recommended)

Step-by-Step: How to Use the 240-Hour Visa-Free Transit

Step 1: Confirm Eligibility

Check if your passport is from one of the 55 eligible countries. Verify your passport has 3+ months validity and 2 blank pages. If your passport does not qualify, apply for a China Tourist Visa instead.

Step 2: Book Your Flights

Book an inbound flight to China and an onward flight to a third country or region within 240 hours. Ensure you arrive at and depart from one of the 60 designated ports.

Step 3: Complete the China Digital Arrival Card

Fill out the free online form on the NIA website or via the China Customs app. You will receive a QR code that must be shown at immigration.

Step 4: Proceed to Immigration on Arrival

At the designated port, proceed to the transit visa-free counter (not the regular visa arrival counter). Present your passport, onward ticket, and Digital Arrival Card. The immigration officer will issue a temporary entry permit stamped for up to 240 hours.

Step 5: Enjoy Your Stay

You can travel within the 24 open provinces. The 240-hour clock starts at midnight after the day of arrival. For example, if you arrive at 3:00 PM on July 10, the 240-hour countdown begins at 12:00 AM on July 11.

Step 6: Depart on Time

Ensure you depart China before the 240-hour limit expires. Overstaying can result in fines, detention, and future entry bans.

Common Reasons for Refusal at Immigration

  • Wrong onward destination (not a third country)
  • Arrival at a non-designated port
  • Passport validity less than 3 months at entry
  • No confirmed onward ticket (transit letters without confirmed seats are rejected)
  • Airline staff refusing boarding due to incomplete documentation

What About Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan?

The 240-hour visa-free transit applies to Mainland China only. Hong Kong and Macau have their own visa-free regimes (most eligible travellers get 90–180 days without a visa). Taiwan has a separate immigration policy.

For the purposes of the "third country" rule, Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan count as separate regions. This means a flight from Mainland China to Hong Kong, Macau, or Taiwan qualifies as a valid onward leg.

China 240-Hour Visa-Free Transit for Indian Passport Holders

As of July 2026, Indian passport holders are not eligible for the China 240 hour visa free transit policy. This China 240 hour visa free for Indians question is the single most common query we receive, and the answer remains clear: there is no China 240 hour visa free for Indians option available under the current rules. Despite China expanding its visa-free transit programme to 55 countries, India is not included in the list.

If you hold an Indian passport, you must apply for a standard China visa before travelling. Here is what you need to do:

China Visa Options for Indian Citizens

Visa Type Purpose Stay Duration
L Visa (Tourist) Tourism, sightseeing Up to 90 days
M Visa (Business) Commercial and trade activities Up to 90 days
Z Visa (Work) Employment in China Based on employment contract
X1/X2 Visa (Student) Study in China X1: >180 days, X2: ≤180 days

For detailed information on visa types, requirements, and the application process, refer to our complete China visa guide for Indians.

How to Apply for a China Visa from India

  1. Complete the COVA online form at the China Online Visa Application (COVA) portal
  2. Gather required documents: Valid passport (6+ months validity, 2+ blank pages), completed visa application form, recent passport-sized photo (33mm x 48mm), travel itinerary, hotel bookings, flight reservations, and supporting documents based on visa type
  3. Submit at CVASC centre: Visit the China Visa Application Service Centre in your jurisdiction (available in Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Kochi)
  4. Pay the visa fee and biometric fee (if applicable)
  5. Track your application using the China visa tracking portal
  6. Collect your passport with the visa sticker

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the China 240-hour visa-free transit policy?

The China 240 hour visa free transit policy allows citizens of 55 eligible countries to enter China and stay for up to 240 hours (10 days) without a visa, provided they hold a confirmed onward ticket to a third country and arrive through a designated port. It replaced the earlier 144-hour scheme in December 2024.

Can Indian passport holders use the 240-hour visa-free transit?

No. Indian passport holders are not eligible for the China visa-free transit policy. Indian citizens must apply for a standard China visa (Tourist L, Business M, etc.) before travelling. Check the China visa guide for Indians for detailed information.

Which countries are eligible for the 240-hour visa-free transit?

55 countries are eligible, including the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, all EU/Schengen countries, Russia, Brazil, UAE, Qatar, and Indonesia. India is not on the list.

How many ports accept the 240-hour visa-free transit?

There are 60 designated ports across China, including major international airports (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Chengdu, Kunming, Xi'an, etc.), selected seaports, and land borders such as the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge.

Can I travel to multiple cities under the 240-hour transit?

Yes. You can travel freely within the 24 open provinces. For example, you can enter through Beijing, travel to Shanghai, visit Xi'an, and depart from Guangzhou, all within 240 hours. You cannot enter the 7 excluded regions (Tibet, Xinjiang, Gansu, Qinghai, Ningxia, Inner Mongolia, Jilin).

Is a return ticket to my home country allowed?

No. The policy requires an onward ticket to a third country or region different from your starting point. A round-trip flight from your home country to China and back does NOT qualify. Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan count as separate regions.

Can I work or study under the 240-hour visa-free transit?

No. The China 240 hour visa free transit strictly prohibits employment, study, and journalistic activities. You may engage in tourism, business meetings, exchange visits, and family visits. For work or study, apply for the appropriate visa (Z visa for work, X visa for study).

When does the 240-hour countdown start?

The 240-hour clock starts at midnight (00:00) after the day of your arrival. If you arrive at 2:00 PM on July 10, your 240-hour period begins at 12:00 AM on July 11 and ends at 12:00 AM on July 21.

Can I extend my stay beyond 240 hours?

Extensions are generally not granted for the visa-free transit policy. If you need to stay longer, you must leave China and re-enter through a designated port (if you still meet the eligibility criteria), or apply for a standard China visa before your trip.

What happens if I overstay the 240-hour limit?

Overstaying can result in fines, detention, and future entry bans. The Chinese immigration authorities strictly enforce the 240-hour limit. If you anticipate needing more time, apply for a standard China visa before travelling.

Does the policy include Hong Kong and Macau?

No, the 240-hour transit China policy applies to Mainland China only. Hong Kong and Macau have their own visa-free arrangements. However, for the transit policy, travelling from Mainland China to Hong Kong, Macau, or Taiwan counts as a valid third-country onward leg.

Conclusion

China's 240-hour visa-free transit policy represents a significant expansion of the country's visa liberalisation efforts, opening up 10-day visa-free stays for citizens of 55 countries across 60 ports and 24 provinces. The policy is free, requires no advance application, and permits travel across multiple provinces, making it one of the most generous transit schemes in Asia.

For Indian passport holders, the key takeaway is clear: you cannot use this policy and must apply for a standard China visa. However, the China visa process is straightforward through the COVA + CVASC system, with application centres in 7 Indian cities.
