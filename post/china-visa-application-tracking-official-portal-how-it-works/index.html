China Visa Application Tracking Official Portal: How It Works

Published: July 7, 2026 7 min read By Ajit Mane

Applying for a China visa is only half the journey—the hardest part is waiting for approval. For Indian applicants, the period between submission and collection can be an anxious time. Fortunately, the China Visa Application Service Center (CVASC) provides a robust online tracking system that lets you monitor your application status in real time.

In this guide, we explain everything about the China visa application tracking official portal—how to track your China visa status online, what each tracking status means, how to interpret delays, and what to do if you see a "Refused" or "Under Review" status for too long. If you are still gathering documents, check our China Visa Documents Checklist for Indians first.

Quick Summary

The official China visa tracking portal is www.visaforchina.org, managed by CVASC. After submitting your application, you receive a stamped receipt with a unique Reference Number. Use this number along with your passport number to check your application status online. Standard processing takes 4-7 working days; premium express processing takes 2-3 working days. For a full overview of China visa types and fees, see our China Visa Guide for Indians.

What Is the China Visa Application Tracking Portal?

The China Visa Application Service Center (CVASC) operates the official online tracking portal at www.visaforchina.org. This is the only authorized platform for tracking China visa applications submitted from India. The portal is managed jointly by CVASC and the Chinese Embassy/Consulates in India.

There are seven CVASC centers across India where you can submit your China visa application:

  • New Delhi – Main center, processes the highest volume of applications
  • Mumbai – Covers applicants from Maharashtra and Gujarat
  • Kolkata – Serves eastern India
  • Chennai – For applicants from Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, and Andhra Pradesh
  • Bengaluru – Newer center for Karnataka residents
  • Hyderabad – Serves Telangana and parts of Andhra Pradesh
  • Ahmedabad – For applicants from Gujarat and surrounding regions

Regardless of which center you apply through, the tracking portal works identically for all Indian applicants.

How to Track Your China Visa Application Online

Tracking your China visa status is a straightforward process. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Locate Your Reference Number

When you submit your China visa application at a CVASC center, the staff will give you a stamped acknowledgment receipt. This receipt contains a unique Reference Number (also called an Application ID or Receipt Number). It typically follows a format like DELI2207XXXXX where the first four letters indicate the application center (DELI = Delhi, MUMI = Mumbai, etc.).

⚠ Save Your Receipt

Take a clear photo of the stamped receipt immediately. The reference number is your only key to tracking the application. Without it, you cannot check your status online and will need to visit the center in person.

Step 2: Visit the Official Tracking Portal

Go to the official CVASC tracking page:

  1. Open www.visaforchina.org
  2. Click on "Track Your Application" (usually at the top or center of the homepage)
  3. Select your application center (New Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, etc.) from the dropdown
  4. Enter your Reference Number exactly as printed on the receipt
  5. Enter your Passport Number (the same one used in the application)
  6. Complete the captcha/security check
  7. Click "Submit" or "Track"

Step 3: Interpret Your Tracking Status

The system will display one of the following statuses. Here is what each one means:

Tracking StatusWhat It MeansWhat to Do
Application SubmittedYour application has been received by the CVASC and is waiting to be forwarded to the embassy/consulate.No action needed. This usually changes within 1 working day.
Under Processing / In ProgressThe Chinese Embassy/Consulate is reviewing your application. This is the most common status and lasts 4-7 working days.Wait patiently. Do not contact the embassy. Excessive inquiries may delay processing.
Additional Documents RequiredThe visa officer needs supplementary documents to make a decision.Check your email (and spam folder) for instructions. Visit the CVASC center with the requested documents within the given timeframe.
Approved / Visa IssuedYour visa has been granted and the visa sticker is being affixed to your passport.Wait for the status to change to "Passport Ready" before visiting the center.
Passport Ready for CollectionYour passport with the visa stamp is ready at the CVASC center for pickup or courier dispatch.Visit the center with your original receipt and ID proof. If you opted for courier, track the shipment.
Refused / DeniedYour visa application has been rejected. The embassy does not typically provide a reason.Collect your passport from the center. You can reapply, but address potential issues (see our rejection guide below).
Passport DispatchedYour passport has been handed over to the courier service for delivery.Use the courier tracking number provided by CVASC to monitor delivery.

When to Expect Status Changes

Most status changes occur between 9:00 AM and 11:00 AM (IST), as the embassy updates the system after morning processing. If you check in the afternoon and the status has not changed, it is likely that there is no update for that day. Avoid checking multiple times a day—once in the morning is sufficient.

What If Tracking Shows "Under Processing" for Too Long?

If your application status remains "Under Processing" for more than 10 working days for standard service (or 7 working days for express), it may indicate one of the following:

  • Background verification – The embassy may be verifying your documents with Indian authorities
  • Additional scrutiny – First-time applicants or those with complex travel histories may face longer processing
  • Peak season backlog – During summer (May-July) and winter holidays (December-January), volumes spike significantly
  • Incomplete documentation – The officer may be waiting for missing documents (though you should receive a notification if this is the case)

If the status does not change for more than 15 working days, take these steps:

  1. Check your email (including spam) for any communication from the CVASC or Chinese Embassy
  2. Call the CVASC helpline at +91-1800-XXX-XXXX (toll-free) with your reference number ready
  3. Visit the CVASC center in person where you submitted your application
  4. Contact the Chinese Embassy in New Delhi as a last resort

What Happens If the Status Shows "Refused"?

If your tracking status changes to "Refused" or "Denied," do not panic. A China visa refusal is not permanent, and many Indian applicants successfully reapply. Here is what you need to know:

⚠ Common Reasons for China Visa Refusal for Indians

  • Insufficient funds – Low bank balance or inconsistent income proof
  • Weak travel history – No previous international travel, especially to developed countries
  • Incomplete documentation – Missing signatures, incorrect photo format, incomplete form
  • Unclear travel purpose – Vague itinerary or inconsistent information in the cover letter
  • Previous visa violations – Overstay or unauthorized work in any country

After a refusal, you have the following options:

  • Collect your passport from the CVASC center (the visa officer does not provide a written reason for refusal)
  • Review and reapply after addressing potential issues. It is advisable to wait at least 2-3 weeks before reapplying
  • Consult a visa expert to understand why your application was rejected. Our consultants at My Visa Assistance can review your documents and provide guidance
  • File an appeal through the Chinese Embassy, though this is rarely successful and reapplying is generally the better option

Tracking Tips for Indian Applicants

Based on years of experience helping Indian applicants with China visas, here are some practical tracking tips:

Expert Tracking Tips

  • Save the receipt number digitally – Take a photo and store it in a secure location. Also note the center name and date of submission
  • Track only once a day – The system updates only once or twice daily. Checking every hour will not speed up the process
  • Bookmark the tracking page – Do not rely on Google searches, as fake/misleading tracking websites exist. Use the official URL only: www.visaforchina.org
  • Use the same passport number – Ensure you enter the exact passport number you used on the application
  • Enable SMS notifications – Some CVASC centers offer SMS updates for a small fee (approx. INR 50-100). This is worth opting for
  • Check spam folder – Status update emails sometimes land in spam. Whitelist @visaforchina.org addresses
  • Do not call the embassy directly – Always contact CVASC first. The embassy does not handle individual tracking inquiries
  • Travel insurance recommended – While tracking, consider purchasing travel insurance that covers visa rejection, so your trip costs are protected

Official Tracking Links & Resources

Always use official channels to track your China visa application. Beware of third-party websites that ask for payment to "expedite" or "track" your visa:

  • Official CVASC Tracking Portal: www.visaforchina.org – The only authorized tracking platform
  • Chinese Embassy in India: in.china-embassy.gov.cn – Official embassy information and consular services
  • Ministry of Foreign Affairs, China: www.cs.mfa.gov.cn – Visa policy updates and official announcements
  • CVASC Helpline: +91-1800-XXX-XXXX (toll-free, Monday to Saturday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM)

🔒 Beware of Scams

Never pay third-party websites that promise to "track" your visa or "influence" the outcome. The official CVASC tracking portal is free to use. Any website asking for payment to check your visa status is a scam. Only use www.visaforchina.org for tracking.

What to Do After Your China Visa Is Approved

Once your tracking status shows "Passport Ready for Collection" or "Passport Dispatched," here is what to do next:

  1. Collect your passport from the CVASC center where you applied (carry the original receipt and a photo ID)
  2. Verify the visa sticker carefully – Check your name, passport number, visa validity dates, number of entries (single, double, or multiple), and duration of stay
  3. Report any errors immediately – If there is a mistake on the visa sticker (e.g., a misspelled name), report it at the CVASC counter before leaving
  4. Make travel arrangements – Book confirmed flights and hotels now that you have the visa
  5. Register with Chinese authorities – If staying for more than 24 hours, your hotel or host must register your accommodation with the local Public Security Bureau (PSB) within 24 hours of arrival

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I track my China visa application without a reference number?

No. The reference number on your CVASC receipt is the primary identifier for tracking. If you lose it, visit the CVASC center where you applied with your passport and a copy of the acknowledgment receipt (if available). The center staff can retrieve your reference number from their records.

Is the China visa tracking portal available 24/7?

Yes, the online tracking portal is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. However, the status information is only updated during working hours (Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM, excluding Chinese and Indian public holidays).

Why is my China visa tracking not showing any result?

This usually happens for one of these reasons: (a) You entered the reference number incorrectly—double-check the format including hyphens; (b) You selected the wrong application center; (c) Your application was just submitted and is not yet in the system—wait 24 hours and try again; (d) The system may be down for maintenance.

Can someone else track my China visa application?

Yes, anyone with your reference number and passport number can check the status. You can share these details with a family member, travel agent, or visa consultant to track on your behalf.

Does tracking status update on weekends?

No. The Chinese Embassy and CVASC centers are closed on Saturdays and Sundays, as well as on Chinese and Indian public holidays. Status updates only occur on working days.

How do I know if my China visa is ready for collection?

When the tracking status changes to "Passport Ready for Collection," your passport is ready. You will also receive an email notification from CVASC if you provided an email address during application. Some centers also send SMS alerts.

Can I track a China visa application submitted through an agent?

Yes. If you applied through a visa agent like My Visa Assistance, they will have your reference number and can track the application for you. However, you can also track it yourself using the same reference number and your passport number on the portal.

The China visa tracking portal is a reliable and transparent system that keeps Indian applicants informed throughout the processing period. By understanding how to use the portal, what each status means, and how to respond to different outcomes, you can manage your application with confidence and reduce the stress of waiting.

  • If refused, review and reapply after 2-3 weeks
