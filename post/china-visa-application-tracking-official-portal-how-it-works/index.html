Check China Visa Status Online – Complete Guide to Application Tracking

Published: July 7, 2026 7 min read By Ajit Mane

Applying for a China visa is only half the journey—the hardest part is waiting for approval. For Indian applicants, the period between submission and visa collection can be an anxious time. Fortunately, the China Visa Application Service Center (CVASC) provides a robust online tracking system that lets you monitor your China visa application status in real time.

In this guide, we explain how to check China visa status online, what each tracking status means, how to check China visa status with passport number, and how to respond to delays or refusal. If you are still gathering documents, read our China Visa Documents Checklist for Indians first.

Quick Summary

The official China visa tracking portal is www.visaforchina.org, managed by CVASC. After submitting your application, you receive a stamped receipt with a unique Reference Number. Use this number along with your passport number to check your China visa application status online. Standard processing takes 4–7 working days; premium express processing takes 2–3 working days. For a full overview of visa types, fees, and documents, see the China Visa Guide for Indians.

What Is the China Visa Application Tracking Portal?

The China Visa Application Service Center (CVASC) operates the official online tracking portal at www.visaforchina.org. This is the only authorized platform for China visa application tracking submitted from India. The portal is managed jointly by CVASC and the Chinese Embassy and Consulates in India.

There are seven CVASC centers across India where you can submit your application:

  • New Delhi – Main center processing the highest volume
  • Mumbai – Covers applicants from Maharashtra and Gujarat
  • Kolkata – Serves eastern India
  • Chennai – For applicants from Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, and Andhra Pradesh
  • Bengaluru – Newer center serving Karnataka residents
  • Hyderabad – Serves Telangana and parts of Andhra Pradesh
  • Ahmedabad – For applicants from Gujarat and surrounding regions

Regardless of which center you apply through, the tracking portal works identically. You can check China visa status from any of these centers using the same online tracking system.

How to Check China Visa Status Online — Step by Step

Learning how to check China visa status is straightforward. Follow these steps to perform a China visa status check online:

Step 1: Locate Your Reference Number

When you submit your visa application at a CVASC center, the staff gives you a stamped acknowledgment receipt containing a unique Reference Number (also called Application ID or Receipt Number). It follows a format like DELI2207XXXXX where the first four letters indicate the center (DELI = Delhi, MUMI = Mumbai, etc.). This number is essential to check China visa status.

⚠ Save Your Receipt

Take a clear photo of the stamped receipt immediately. Without the reference number, you cannot perform a China visa status check online and will need to visit the center in person to retrieve it.

Step 2: Visit the Official CVASC Tracking Portal

Go to the official CVASC tracking page and follow these steps:

  1. Open www.visaforchina.org
  2. Click "Track Your Application" on the homepage
  3. Select your application center (New Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, etc.)
  4. Enter your Reference Number exactly as printed on the receipt
  5. To check China visa status with passport number, enter your passport number exactly as used in the application
  6. Complete the captcha/security check
  7. Click "Submit"

How to Check China Visa Status with Passport Number

Many Indian applicants ask: can I check China visa status with passport number alone? The answer is yes, but you also need the reference number from your CVASC receipt. The portal requires both your passport number and reference number to retrieve your China visa status. To check China visa status with passport number, simply enter the same passport number you used on the application form. Ensure there are no typos—even a single digit mismatch will prevent the portal from finding your China visa application status.

ⓘ Passport Number Tips

  • Use the exact passport number from your application form (including any letters)
  • Do not add spaces or hyphens
  • Ensure the passport has at least 6 months validity remaining
  • If you renewed your passport after applying, the old passport number is still valid for tracking

When Do Status Updates Occur?

Most China visa status updates occur between 9:00 AM and 11:00 AM (IST), after the embassy updates the system. Check once in the morning—excessive checking will not speed up processing. Track once daily using the official portal.

China Visa Status Not Changing? What to Do

If your China visa application status remains unchanged for more than 10 working days (standard service) or 7 working days (express), it may indicate:

  • Background verification – The embassy may be verifying your documents with Indian authorities
  • Additional scrutiny – First-time applicants or those with complex travel histories face longer processing
  • Peak season backlog – Summer (May–July) and winter holidays (December–January) see high volumes
  • Incomplete documentation – The officer may need missing documents (you should receive an email if so)

⚠ Tracking Delayed Beyond 10 Days?

  1. Check your email (including spam) for CVASC or embassy communication
  2. Call the CVASC helpline with your reference number ready
  3. Visit your CVASC center in person where you submitted the application
  4. Contact the Chinese Embassy in New Delhi as a last resort

For a complete document checklist to ensure your application is complete before resubmission, refer to the China Visa Documents 2026 Checklist and China Visa Requirements page.

What Each China Visa Tracking Status Means in Detail

Application Submitted

Your documents have been accepted by CVASC. The reference number on your receipt is now active. This status usually changes within 24 hours once the embassy receives your file.

Under Processing

Your application is with the Chinese Embassy or Consulate for review. The visa officer is verifying your documents, travel history, and purpose of visit. This is the longest phase and typically lasts 4–7 working days. Some categories such as China tourist visa (L) and China business visa (M) may process faster than China work visa (Z) or China student visa (X1/X2), which may require additional verification (see our China Visa Guide for type-specific timelines).

Additional Documents Required

Occasionally, the visa officer requests more information. This is not a refusal—respond promptly with the requested documents. Common requests include updated bank statements, confirmed flight itineraries, or a detailed cover letter explaining travel purpose.

Approved / Visa Issued

Your visa has been approved. The visa sticker is being printed and affixed to your passport. Do not visit the CVASC center yet—wait for the status to change to "Passport Ready for Collection."

Passport Ready for Collection

Your passport with the visa is ready for pickup. Visit your CVASC center with the original receipt and a government-issued photo ID. If you opted for courier delivery, your passport will be dispatched within 24–48 hours.

Passport Dispatched

Your passport has been handed to the courier service. Use the tracking number provided by CVASC to monitor its delivery to your address.

Refused

Your visa application has been denied. The embassy does not provide a written reason, but common causes for Indian applicants include insufficient funds, weak travel history, incomplete documentation, unclear travel purpose, or previous visa violations. Collect your passport, review the China visa documents checklist to identify gaps, and consider reapplying after addressing the issues. Many Indian applicants successfully obtain a China visa on their second attempt.

China Visa Tracking Tips for Indian Applicants

Expert Tips for a Smooth Tracking Experience

  • Save your reference number digitally – Take a photo and store it securely
  • Track once daily – The system updates only once or twice per day
  • Use only the official portal – www.visaforchina.org – avoid third-party tracking sites that charge money
  • Enter the exact passport number – to check China visa status with passport number, ensure no typos
  • Enable SMS notifications – Some CVASC centers offer SMS updates for INR 50–100
  • Check your spam folder – whitelist @visaforchina.org addresses
  • Contact CVASC first – the embassy does not handle individual tracking inquiries
  • Purchase travel insurance that covers visa rejection to protect your trip costs

What to Do After Your China Visa Is Approved

Once your China visa tracking shows "Passport Ready for Collection" or "Passport Dispatched":

  1. Collect your passport from the CVASC center with your original receipt and photo ID
  2. Verify the visa sticker – Check your name, passport number, validity dates, entries, and duration of stay
  3. Report errors immediately at the CVASC counter if you find a mistake
  4. Book travel arrangements with confirmed flights and hotels
  5. Register with local authorities in China within 24 hours of arrival (your hotel will handle this)

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check my China visa application status?

Visit the official CVASC tracking portal at www.visaforchina.org, select your application center, enter your reference number and passport number, complete the captcha, and click Submit. Your current China visa application status will be displayed instantly.

Can I check China visa status online?

Yes. The CVASC portal provides a free China visa status check online service available 24/7. You need your reference number (from the CVASC receipt) and passport number to access your status.

Can I check China visa status using my passport number?

Yes, you can check China visa status with passport number, but you also need the reference number from your CVASC receipt. The portal requires both details to locate your application. Enter your passport number exactly as it appears on your application form.

How long does China visa processing take?

Standard processing takes 4–7 working days. Express service takes 3–4 working days, and premium rush service takes 2–3 working days. Additional verification may extend processing to 10+ working days.

What does "Under Processing" mean?

"Under Processing" means your application is with the Chinese Embassy or Consulate for review. The visa officer is verifying your documents, travel history, and purpose of visit. This is the most common status and typically lasts 4–7 working days for standard applications.

What does "Ready for Collection" mean?

"Passport Ready for Collection" means your passport with the visa stamp is ready at the CVASC center. Visit the center with your original receipt and a government-issued photo ID during working hours. If you opted for courier, your passport will be dispatched within 24–48 hours.

What should I do if my application is delayed?

If your status remains unchanged for more than 10 working days, first check your email (including spam) for CVASC communication. Call the CVASC helpline with your reference number, or visit your application center in person. Avoid contacting the Chinese Embassy directly—CVASC handles all tracking inquiries.

Can I track China visa status without a reference number?

No. The reference number on your CVASC receipt is the primary identifier for China visa application tracking. If you lose it, visit the CVASC center where you applied with your passport. Staff can retrieve your reference number from their records.

Does the tracking portal work on weekends?

The portal is available 24/7, but status information only updates on working days (Monday to Friday, 9 AM–5 PM, excluding Chinese and Indian public holidays).

Can someone else track my application on my behalf?

Yes. Anyone with your reference number and passport number can check China visa status on your behalf. Share these details with a family member, travel agent, or visa consultant to track your application.
