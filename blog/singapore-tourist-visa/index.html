How to Apply for a Singapore Tourist Visa 2026: Process, Fees & Requirements

Published: June 30, 2026 12 min read By My Visa Assistance Team

Singapore, the Lion City, is one of the most popular travel destinations in Asia. Known for its futuristic skyline, lush gardens, world-class shopping, and mouth-watering culinary scene, Singapore attracts millions of tourists every year. For Indian travellers and citizens of many other countries, obtaining a Singapore Tourist Visa is a straightforward process if you follow the correct steps.

In this comprehensive guide, we cover everything you need to know about the Singapore tourist visa application process for 2026 — from eligibility criteria and required documents to visa fees, processing time, and essential travel tips. Whether you're planning a leisure trip, visiting family, or exploring Singapore for business, this guide will walk you through the entire process.

For more information on visa requirements for other destinations, visit our comprehensive Visa Guide.

Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Singapore?

Yes, Indian citizens require a valid visa to enter Singapore for tourism, business, or social visits. However, starting from February 2024, Indians holding valid US visas, multiple-entry Schengen visas, or certain other long-term visas can use the Visa-Free Transit Facility (VFTF) for up to 96 hours under specific conditions. For most travellers, applying for a Singapore tourist visa well in advance of your trip is the recommended approach.

Singapore offers the following visa options for Indian nationals:

  • Tourist Visa (short-term visit pass) — for tourism, family visits, and social purposes
  • Business Visa — for business meetings, conferences, and professional engagements
  • Transit Visa — for short layovers (covers 96 hours VFTF under qualifying conditions)

Important Note

Singapore does not currently offer an e-Visa for Indian nationals. Applications must be submitted through a Singapore Overseas Mission, an authorised visa agent in India, or a local contact/sponsor in Singapore via the SAVE (Submission of Visa Application Electronically) system. For more information, visit the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA) website.

Eligibility Criteria for Singapore Tourist Visa

Before starting your application, ensure you meet the following basic eligibility requirements:

  • Valid Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months from the date of intended entry into Singapore, with at least two blank pages for visa stamping.
  • Purpose of Visit: Must be for tourism, social visit, or medical treatment. You must demonstrate genuine intent to return to your home country after the visit.
  • Sufficient Funds: Must have adequate financial means to cover your stay in Singapore (typically at least SGD 1,000-1,500 for a short visit).
  • Confirmed Travel Itinerary: Return/onward flight ticket and confirmed hotel accommodation.
  • Good Character: No criminal record or previous immigration violations in Singapore or other countries.
  • No Prior Visa Refusals: Applicants with a history of visa refusal from Singapore may face additional scrutiny.

Who Can Apply?

The Singapore tourist visa is available to citizens of countries that require a visa for entry, including India, China, Myanmar, Indonesia (non-visa-free), Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Russia, CIS countries, the Middle East, Africa, and parts of South America. For the full list of visa-required countries, check the ICA website.

Pro Tip: If you are an Indian national holding a valid US, UK, or Schengen visa, you may be eligible for Singapore's Visa-Free Transit Facility (VFTF) for up to 96 hours. However, this is at the discretion of the immigration officer at the checkpoint.

Required Documents for Singapore Tourist Visa

Submitting a complete and accurate document set is critical for a successful visa application. Below is the complete checklist:

Core Documents

  • Form 14A (Visa Application Form): The official Singapore visa application form, fully completed and signed. You can download it directly from the ICA website.
  • Valid Passport: Original passport with minimum 6 months validity and at least 2 blank pages. Include copies of all pages with stamps, endorsements, and the bio-data page.
  • Recent Passport-Sized Photographs: Two recent (within 3 months), colour photographs on a white background. Dimensions: 35mm x 45mm. The face should cover about 70-80% of the photo.
  • Travel Itinerary: Confirmed return/onward flight booking and hotel reservations for the entire duration of stay.
  • Bank Statements: Last 3-6 months of personal bank statements with sufficient balance (minimum SGD 1,500-2,000 or equivalent). Salary slips and income tax returns (ITR) for the last 2-3 years add credibility.
  • Cover Letter: A letter explaining the purpose of your visit, travel dates, places you plan to visit, and assurance of return to home country.
  • Proof of Employment/Leave: Employment letter, NOC from employer, or leave approval letter (for employed applicants). For students: university ID card and NOC from institution.

Additional Documents (if applicable)

  • Invitation Letter: If visiting family/friends in Singapore, a letter of invitation from your host with their SingPass, work pass, or NRIC details.
  • Hotel Confirmation: If not staying with a local contact, confirmed hotel booking for the entire stay.
  • Travel Insurance: Although not mandatory for a tourist visa, travel insurance is highly recommended to cover medical emergencies, trip cancellation, and lost baggage.
  • Marriage Certificate: If married and your spouse is not travelling with you.
  • Previous Visas: Copies of any previous visas for Singapore or other countries (US, UK, Schengen, Australia) to strengthen your application.
Important: All documents not in English must be translated by a certified translator. Submit both the original and the translated copies. Incomplete documentation is the #1 reason for visa delays and rejections.

Step-by-Step Application Process for Singapore Tourist Visa

Follow these steps to apply for your Singapore Tourist Visa in 2026:

1

Determine Your Application Channel

Choose how you will apply: (a) Through an authorised visa agent in India (recommended for most applicants), (b) Through a local contact/sponsor in Singapore via the SAVE system, or (c) At the Singapore High Commission/Embassy in your country. For Indian applicants, using an authorised visa agent is the most convenient method.

2

Complete Form 14A

Download and fill out Form 14A (see download section above). Fill in all sections accurately. The form asks for personal details, passport information, travel itinerary, accommodation details, employment information, and declarations. Sign and date the form.

3

Gather Supporting Documents

Collect all required documents as per the checklist above. Ensure your passport has at least 6 months validity, your photos meet the specifications, and your bank statements show sufficient funds. Organise documents in the order specified by your application channel.

4

Submit Your Application

Submit your completed Form 14A along with all supporting documents to your chosen channel. If applying through an authorised agent, they will upload your application via the SAVE system. If a local sponsor is applying on your behalf, they will submit through the SAVE portal using their SingPass.

5

Pay the Visa Fee

The non-refundable visa processing fee is SGD 30 (approximately INR 1,850). This is paid at the time of application submission. Some authorised agents may charge additional service fees (typically INR 1,000-2,000) for handling the application.

6

Wait for Processing

Standard processing time is 3-5 working days. Some applications may take longer (up to 2 weeks) if additional verification is required. You can check the status of your application through the e-Service portal on the ICA website if your application was submitted via SAVE.

7

Collect Your Visa

Once approved, your visa will be issued as a digital e-Visa sent to your email or as a physical visa sticker (depending on the application channel). The e-Visa will contain your personal details, visa validity dates, number of entries permitted, and maximum stay per visit.

Singapore Tourist Visa Fees (2026)

The fee structure for a Singapore tourist visa is standardised:

Fee Component Amount Notes
Visa Processing Fee SGD 30 Non-refundable, paid at submission
Authorised Agent Service Fee (if applicable) INR 1,000 - 2,000 Varies by agent/agency
SAVE System Fee (if sponsor applies) SGD 30 Paid online via the SAVE portal
Visa-on-Arrival (if eligible) Varies Only available for specific nationalities

Fee Exemptions

The SGD 30 visa fee is exempted for certain categories, including children under 6 years old, nationals of specified countries on official business, and holders of diplomatic or official passports (subject to bilateral agreements). Check with the Singapore Overseas Mission in your country for specific exemptions.

Processing Time for Singapore Tourist Visa

Processing times can vary based on the application channel and workload at the Singapore Overseas Mission:

Application Channel Standard Processing Expedited/Priority
Authorised Visa Agent (India) 3-5 working days Not available
SAVE System (Local Sponsor) 3-5 working days Not available
Singapore High Commission (Direct) 5-10 working days Not available
Singapore Overseas Mission (Other countries) 5-10 working days Not available
Pro Tip: Apply at least 2-3 weeks before your intended travel date to allow sufficient processing time. Peak travel seasons (December, Chinese New Year, June holidays, Diwali) may see longer processing times due to higher application volumes.

Singapore Visa Validity and Stay Duration

Visa Type Validity Max Stay Per Visit Entries
Tourist Visa (Short-term Visit Pass) Up to 2 years (discretionary) 30 days Single / Multiple (discretionary)
Business Visa Up to 2 years (discretionary) 30 days Multiple (typically)

Note: The visa validity and number of entries are at the discretion of the ICA officer. First-time applicants are often issued a single-entry visa valid for 1 month. Frequent travellers with a good travel history may receive multiple-entry visas valid for up to 2 years.

Travel Tips for Singapore

Once you have your visa approved, here are some essential travel tips to make your Singapore trip smooth and enjoyable:

Carry Sufficient Funds

Immigration officers may ask to see proof of sufficient funds. Carry at least SGD 1,000-1,500 for a short visit. Credit cards are widely accepted, but having some Singapore Dollars in cash is advisable for small vendors and taxis.

Keep Digital Copy of Your Visa

Save a digital copy of your approved e-Visa on your phone and carry a printed copy in your hand luggage. You may need to show it at airline check-in counters and at Singapore immigration.

Download Useful Apps

Download MyTransport.SG (for public transport), Grab (ride-hailing), Visit Singapore (tourist guide), and SG Arrival Card (for digital arrival declaration before entry) apps before your trip.

Follow Singapore's Strict Laws

Singapore has strict laws that visitors must follow. Chewing gum is banned (except medicinal), smoking is restricted to designated areas, and strict penalties apply for littering, jaywalking, and importing controlled substances. Always carry your passport with you as a form of identification.

Plan for the Weather

Singapore is hot and humid year-round (average 28-32°C). Carry light cotton clothing, a hat, sunscreen, and an umbrella. Afternoon thunderstorms are common, so plan indoor activities accordingly.

Use Public Transport

Singapore's MRT (Mass Rapid Transit) system is efficient, clean, and affordable. Get an EZ-Link or Singapore Tourist Pass for unlimited travel. Taxis and ride-hailing apps are also available but cost more.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get a Singapore tourist visa?

Standard processing takes 3-5 working days for applications submitted through authorised agents or the SAVE system. Direct applications to the Singapore High Commission may take 5-10 working days.

Can I apply for a Singapore visa online?

Singapore does not have a direct e-Visa portal for all nationalities. Applications must be submitted through the SAVE system by an authorised visa agent or a local sponsor in Singapore. Individual online submission is not available for most tourist visa applicants.

What is Form 14A and where do I get it?

Form 14A is the official Singapore visa application form prescribed by the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA). You can download it directly from the ICA website. It is mandatory for all tourist visa applications.

Is the Singapore visa fee refundable if rejected?

No, the SGD 30 visa processing fee is non-refundable regardless of the outcome of your application. The fee covers the administrative cost of processing your application.

Can I extend my stay in Singapore beyond 30 days?

You may apply for a Visit Pass Extension through the ICA e-Service portal before your current visit pass expires. Extensions are granted on a case-by-case basis and typically allow up to 30 additional days. An extension fee of SGD 40 applies.

Do I need travel insurance for a Singapore tourist visa?

Travel insurance is not mandatory for a Singapore tourist visa application, but it is highly recommended. Medical costs in Singapore are among the highest in the world, and travel insurance covers medical emergencies, trip cancellation, lost baggage, and other contingencies.

Can I work on a Singapore tourist visa?

No, a tourist visa strictly prohibits employment or paid work in Singapore. If you intend to work, you must apply for the appropriate Employment Pass, S Pass, or Work Permit through your employer.

What should I do if my Singapore visa is rejected?

If your visa is rejected, you have the following options:

  • Check the reason for rejection and address the gaps (e.g., insufficient funds, incomplete documents, weak travel history).
  • Re-apply after addressing the deficiencies, but note that repeated applications without changes to your circumstances may still be rejected.
  • Appeal the decision through your authorised agent or sponsor. Appeals must include new supporting documents that address the rejection reasons.
  • Consider applying through a different channel (e.g., through a local sponsor if you previously applied via an agent).

Conclusion

Applying for a Singapore Tourist Visa in 2026 is a well-defined process that requires careful document preparation and attention to detail. By following the step-by-step guide outlined above, completing Form 14A accurately, and submitting a complete set of supporting documents, you significantly increase your chances of a smooth and successful visa application.

Remember to apply at least 2-3 weeks before your planned travel date, carry all required documents when travelling, and follow Singapore's laws and regulations during your stay. Singapore is a beautiful, safe, and vibrant city-state that offers an unforgettable travel experience.
