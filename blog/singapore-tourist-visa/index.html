How to Apply for a Singapore Tourist Visa 2026: Process, Fees & Requirements

Published: June 30, 2026 15 min read By My Visa Assistance Team

Singapore, the Lion City, is one of Asia's most dynamic travel destinations. Whether you're visiting for tourism, business, or transit, understanding Singapore's entry requirements is essential for a smooth journey. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about the Singapore tourist visa process in 2026, including general entry rules, visa exemption policies, application routes, required documents, fees, processing times, and practical travel tips.

Executive Summary

Singapore entry requires a valid passport (â‰¥6 months validity) and an SG Arrival Card (submitted online within 3 days before arrival). Visa-exempt nationals (e.g. US, EU, AUS, NZ, UK, Japan, South Korea, etc.) may visit visa-free for up to 30â€“90 days. Citizens of visa-required countries must obtain an entry visa in advance. Visa applications can be submitted online via ICA's e-Service (SAVE) by a Singapore local contact (with SingPass ID) for a SGD 30 fee, or through authorised visa centres (e.g. VFS Global or diplomatic missions) where additional service fees apply. Processing typically takes 3â€“8 working days. Upon arrival, immigration officers issue a Visit Pass (valid for the authorised stay, not tied to visa validity). Visitors must show onward tickets, sufficient funds, and (if applicable) visas for next destinations. Overstays are strictly penalised. Recent changes include fully digital Visit Pass (e-Pass) notices since 2022 and the lifting of COVID-era entry rules by 2023.

General Entry Requirements

Passport & Travel Document

Each traveller needs an individual passport with at least 6 months' validity beyond the intended departure date from Singapore. Collective or group passports are not accepted. Refugees or alien passport holders require special assessment on a case-by-case basis.

SG Arrival Card (SGAC)

All visitors must submit the SG Arrival Card (including an electronic health declaration) online via ICA's e-service or the MyICA mobile app within 3 days before arrival. Submission is free; failure to do so can result in denied entry. Transit passengers who do not clear immigration and Singapore residents entering via land checkpoints are exempt from this requirement.

Funds & Itinerary

Travellers should have sufficient funds for their stay and confirmed onward/return tickets. Although no fixed amount is published, ICA advises carrying proof of cash, credit cards, or bank statements. Your travel itinerary and hotel bookings should be readily available, as immigration officers may verify your plans upon arrival.

Health & Customs

Yellow Fever vaccination proof is required if arriving from an affected country. All baggage and items must comply with Singapore's customs rules; cash amounts exceeding SGD 20,000 (or equivalent in foreign currency) must be declared.

Visa Exemption vs. Requirement

Visa-Exempt Nationals

Citizens of many countries, including the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Australia, Japan, all EU member states, South Korea, and most ASEAN countries, do not need a pre-arranged visa for short stays. Typical permitted stay durations are:

  • US citizens: Up to 90 days
  • UK citizens: Up to 30â€“90 days (at immigration officer's discretion)
  • Australian, Canadian, Japanese, South Korean citizens: Up to 90 days
  • EU/Schengen area citizens: Up to 90 days
  • Most ASEAN nationals: Up to 30 days

Visa-exempt travellers still must meet all entry criteria (passport validity, SG Arrival Card, sufficient funds, onward ticket). No visa application steps or fees are involved.

Visa-Required Nationals

Passport holders from approximately 40+ countries must obtain an Entry Visa before travelling to Singapore. These include India, China, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Nigeria, Ghana, Russia, CIS countries, many Middle Eastern and African nations. Check the official ICA "Check if You Need a Visa" page for the complete list. Possessing a valid visa does not guarantee entry; visa holders must still meet all entry requirements upon arrival.

Visa-Free Transit Facility (VFTF)

Certain nationals (including India, CIS states, Georgia, Turkmenistan, Ukraine, and selected PRC document holders) can transit visa-free for up to 96 hours if travelling onward to a third country. Conditions include a valid onward ticket confirming departure within 96 hours and transit by air or sea. No extensions are permitted under VFTF. For example, an Indian passport holder with a valid US visa transiting Singapore en route to the USA may qualify.

Important Note

Singapore does not currently offer a direct e-Visa portal for all nationalities. Applications must be submitted through the SAVE system by an authorised visa agent or a local sponsor in Singapore (with SingPass ID). Individual online submission is not available for most tourist visa applicants. For more information, visit the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA) website.

Application Routes & Procedures

ICA e-Service (SAVE) — Local Sponsor Route

For visa-required travellers who have a Singapore citizen or Permanent Resident (PR) contact, the ICA e-Service (SAVE) is the primary route. The local contact (â‰¥21 years old with a SingPass ID) submits the visa application online up to 30 days before travel. A non-refundable SGD 30 visa fee is charged to the sponsor. The process requires uploading digital copies of documents (Form 14A, passport bio-page, photograph, itinerary, sponsor's letter). Processing typically takes 3 working days. On approval, an e-Visa is issued (print out recommended for travel). Upon arrival, the visitor receives an electronic Visit Pass (e-Pass) by email.

Authorised Visa Centres & Diplomatic Missions

Applicants without a local sponsor must apply through an authorised visa agent or a Singapore diplomatic mission. For example:

  • VFS Global: Operates centres in many countries (e.g. Australia, Indonesia, China). Charges an additional service fee (e.g. AUD 77 in Australia) plus the SGD 30 visa fee.
  • Singapore Embassy/High Commission: Applicants can submit by mail or in person at the nearest Singapore diplomatic mission. Embassy service hours and fees vary (e.g. US Embassy charges USD 23 for processing).
  • Mail-in Applications: Some missions accept applications by courier with a self-addressed return envelope.

Required documents are similar but submitted in paper format. Processing ranges from 5â€“8 business days depending on the mission's workload.

Visa-Free Entry

If no visa is required for your nationality, you skip the ICA application entirely. Simply submit your SG Arrival Card within 3 days before arrival and ensure all documents (passport, itinerary, funds) are in order. Upon arrival, you will receive a Visit Pass (stamp or e-notification) for the permitted stay duration.

Application Process Step-by-Step

1

Check Your Visa Requirement

Visit the official ICA website to determine whether your nationality requires a visa. Check your passport validity (â‰¥6 months) and confirm your eligibility.

2

Choose Your Application Channel

Decide how you will apply: (a) Through a local sponsor via the ICA e-Service (SAVE), (b) Through an authorised visa agent like VFS Global, or (c) At the nearest Singapore diplomatic mission. The SAVE route is the fastest and most affordable.

3

Complete Form 14A

Download Form 14A from the ICA website. Fill in all sections accurately, including personal details, passport information, travel itinerary, accommodation details, employment information, and declarations. Sign and date the form.

4

Gather Supporting Documents

Collect all required documents as per the checklist below. Ensure your passport has â‰¥6 months validity, your photos meet specifications (35mm Ã— 45mm, white background), and your bank statements demonstrate sufficient funds.

5

Submit Your Application

Submit your completed Form 14A along with supporting documents through your chosen channel. For the SAVE route, your sponsor uploads everything online. For missions and agencies, submit physical copies.

6

Pay the Visa Fee

The non-refundable visa processing fee is SGD 30 (approximately INR 1,850). This is paid at the time of submission. Authorised agents may charge additional service fees (typically INR 1,000â€“2,000 or equivalent).

7

Wait for Processing

Standard processing takes 3â€“8 working days depending on the application channel and current workload. Check your application status through the ICA e-Service portal or your agent.

8

Receive Your Visa

Once approved, your visa is issued as a digital e-Visa sent to your email (or provided through your agent). Carry a printed copy when travelling. Upon arrival, you will receive an electronic Visit Pass (e-Pass) by email.

Required Supporting Documents

Applicants must submit the following standard documents (digital copies for the e-Service; originals at missions):

  • Passport: Original passport (â‰¥6 months' validity) plus copies of bio-data page.
  • Application Form 14A: Completed Singapore visa form (IMM-14A), including personal details, travel dates, purpose, and stay length. Indicate the type of visa requested: single, double, or multiple journey.
  • Photographs: Two recent passport-sized colour photos (white background, 35mm Ã— 45mm, head straight, neutral expression, taken within 3 months).
  • Itinerary & Bookings: Confirmed or provisional flight itinerary and hotel or accommodation details.
  • Proof of Funds: Bank statements for the last 3â€“6 months, payslips, or an employment letter. ICA expects evidence of sufficient funds for the intended stay.
  • Cover Letter: A one-page letter explaining the trip purpose, dates, itinerary, and applicant's ties to their home country.
  • Local Contact Letter (V39A): Some nationalities require a Letter of Introduction from a Singapore contact or company. The ICA V39A form provides a template.
  • Proof of Employment/Leave: Employment letter, NOC from employer, or leave approval letter. For students: university ID and NOC from institution.
  • Next-Destination Visa (if applicable): If travelling onward, include evidence of visas for subsequent destinations.

Additional Documents (if applicable)

  • Invitation Letter: If visiting family/friends, a letter of invitation from your host with their SingPass, work pass, or NRIC details.
  • Travel Insurance: Not mandatory but highly recommended to cover medical emergencies and trip cancellation.
  • Birth Certificate & Parental Consent: Required for minors (especially if travelling with only one parent).
  • Marriage Certificate: If married and your spouse is not travelling with you.
  • Previous Visas: Copies of any previous visas for Singapore or other countries (US, UK, Schengen, Australia) to strengthen your application.
Important: All documents not in English must be translated by a certified translator. Submit both the original and the translated copies. Incomplete documentation is the #1 reason for visa delays and rejections.

Validity & Extensions

The issued Visit Pass on arrival determines the permitted stay. This is generally 30 days (extendable once for valid reasons) or longer (up to 90 days) for certain nationalities. Extensions can be requested via the ICA e-Service if more time is needed. An extension fee of SGD 40 applies.

Multiple vs. Single Entry

In the e-Visa application form, you can request single, double, triple, or multiple journey visas. Single-entry visas permit one entry; multiple-entry visas (rarely issued for tourists) allow multiple entries within the visa validity period. If you need to exit and re-enter Singapore (e.g. a short trip to Johor, Malaysia and back), request a Double or Triple Journey visa.

Peak travel seasons (December, Chinese New Year, June holidays, Diwali) may see longer processing times due to higher application volumes. There is no expedited track available for Singapore tourist visas.

Common Refusal Reasons & Tips

Typical grounds for visa denial include: missing or incomplete documents, insufficient proof of funds, inconsistent or false information, weak travel itinerary, short passport validity, or previous overstays in Singapore. To mitigate these risks:

  • Complete Application: Double-check all forms are fully filled, signed, and attached. Avoid discrepancies in name spellings and dates.
  • Document Clarity: Provide clear, annotated copies (and originals if required). Follow ICA's photo guidelines for headshots.
  • Funds & Ties: Demonstrate strong ties to your home country (stable job, family) and sufficient funds. Bank statements should show regular income. If large deposits appear, include an explanation letter.
  • Consistent Itinerary: Ensure flight and hotel bookings match the dates on your forms and cover letter. Avoid contradictory or fake reservations.
  • Legal Record: Disclose any criminal history; some offences (even minor) can lead to denial.
  • Stay Informed: Check ICA advisories regularly for current rules and any new security screening policies.

Recent Policy Changes (2023â€“2026)

Digital Visit Pass (e-Pass)

Since 11 March 2022, Singapore no longer stamps passports for short-term visitors. Approved visit passes are sent electronically via email upon arrival. Applicants must provide a valid email address in the SG Arrival Card for e-Pass delivery.

SG Arrival Card Evolution

The SG Arrival Card (with health declaration) replaced the paper Disembarkation Card in 2020. It remains mandatory for all visitors, with health-related questions for disease control purposes.

COVID-19 Rules Lifted

As of February 2023, all COVID-19 entry restrictions were removed. There are no testing, quarantine, or vaccination requirements. Mask mandates on public transport ended in February 2023. Only the SG Arrival Card remains in place.

Advanced Passenger Screening (2026)

From 30 January 2026, airlines must submit passenger data to ICA for pre-screening. Passengers flagged as "high-risk" could receive a no-boarding notice. Affected travellers must resolve issues through ICA's feedback portal before booking flights.

Travel Tips for Singapore

Carry Sufficient Funds

Immigration officers may ask to see proof of sufficient funds. Carry at least SGD 1,000â€“1,500 for a short visit. Credit cards are widely accepted, but having some Singapore Dollars in cash is advisable for small vendors and taxis.

Keep Digital and Printed Copies of Your Visa

Save a digital copy of your approved e-Visa on your phone and carry a printed copy in your hand luggage. You may need to show it at airline check-in counters and at Singapore immigration.

Download Useful Apps

Download MyTransport.SG (for public transport), Grab (ride-hailing), Visit Singapore (tourist guide), and the MyICA app (for SG Arrival Card and immigration services) before your trip.

Follow Singapore's Strict Laws

Singapore has strict laws that visitors must follow. Chewing gum is banned (except medicinal), smoking is restricted to designated areas, and strict penalties apply for littering, jaywalking, and importing controlled substances. Always carry your passport as identification.

Plan for the Weather

Singapore is hot and humid year-round (average 28â€“32Â°C). Carry light cotton clothing, a hat, sunscreen, and an umbrella. Afternoon thunderstorms are common, so plan indoor activities accordingly.

Use Public Transport

Singapore's MRT (Mass Rapid Transit) system is efficient, clean, and affordable. Get an EZ-Link or Singapore Tourist Pass for unlimited travel. Taxis and ride-hailing apps like Grab are also available.

Special Categories

Families

Each traveller (including children) needs a separate visa application and receives an individual Visit Pass. A family cover letter listing all members, relationships, and shared itinerary is recommended. Children under 12 should have their details included on the adult's form or their own form. Accompanying spouses should submit marriage certificates if required.

Minors

Children's passports and birth certificates must be included with the application. A Parental Consent Letter is advisable if only one parent is travelling with the child. The visa form requires details of a "travelling companion" for minors.

Elderly & Retired Travellers

No additional visa forms are required based on age. However, applicants with medical conditions may provide a doctor's letter or travel insurance. The itinerary should not be too taxing for seniors. Showing stable retirement income or pension statements can substitute for employment proof.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get a Singapore tourist visa?

Standard processing takes 3â€“5 working days for applications submitted through the SAVE system, and 5â€“10 working days for applications submitted through diplomatic missions or authorised agents.

Can I apply for a Singapore visa online myself?

Singapore does not have a direct e-Visa portal for individual applicants. Applications must be submitted through the SAVE system by an authorised visa agent or a local sponsor in Singapore. Individual online submission is not available for most tourist visa applicants.

What is Form 14A and where do I get it?

Form 14A is the official Singapore visa application form prescribed by ICA. You can download it directly from the ICA website. It is mandatory for all tourist visa applications.

Is the Singapore visa fee refundable if rejected?

No, the SGD 30 visa processing fee is non-refundable regardless of the outcome of your application. The fee covers the administrative processing cost.

Can I extend my stay in Singapore beyond 30 days?

You may apply for a Visit Pass Extension through the ICA e-Service portal before your current visit pass expires. Extensions are granted on a case-by-case basis and typically allow up to 30 additional days. An extension fee of SGD 40 applies.

Do I need travel insurance for a Singapore tourist visa?

Travel insurance is not mandatory for a Singapore tourist visa application, but it is highly recommended. Medical costs in Singapore are among the highest in the world, and travel insurance covers medical emergencies, trip cancellation, lost baggage, and other contingencies.

Can I work on a Singapore tourist visa?

No, a tourist visa strictly prohibits employment or paid work in Singapore. You must apply for the appropriate Employment Pass, S Pass, or Work Permit through a Singapore employer.

What should I do if my Singapore visa is rejected?

If your visa is rejected, consider the following steps:

  • Check the reason for rejection and address the gaps (e.g., insufficient funds, incomplete documents, weak travel history).
  • Re-apply after addressing the deficiencies. Repeated applications without substantive changes may still be rejected.
  • Appeal the decision through your authorised agent or sponsor. Appeals must include new supporting documents addressing the rejection reasons.
  • Consider applying through a different channel (e.g., through a local sponsor if you previously applied via an agent).
