Henley Passport Index 2026: India's Rank, Visa-Free Score, and How to Improve Travel Freedom

Published: June 10, 2026 7 min read By My Visa Assistance Team Updated: June 10, 2026

For Indian travellers, the passport you hold determines how freely you can explore the world. The Henley Passport Index 2026 offers the most authoritative global ranking of passports based on the number of destinations accessible without a prior visa. This guide breaks down India's current rank, visa-free score, the strongest and weakest passports globally, and actionable strategies to improve your travel freedom.

What is the Henley Passport Index?

The Henley Passport Index is published by Henley & Partners, a global citizenship and residency advisory firm. It ranks 199 passports against 227 travel destinations using exclusive data from the International Air Transport Association (IATA). The index is updated quarterly, making it the most up-to-date passport ranking in the world.

The score reflects the number of destinations the passport holder can visit visa-free, with a visa on arrival, through an eVisa, or via an electronic travel authorisation (ETA). Destinations requiring a traditional visa do not count toward the score.

Methodology Highlights

Each passport is assigned a score equal to the number of destinations the holder can enter without a traditional visa. The data is cross-checked with IATA, government sources, and on-the-ground reports. Ties are broken by the number of visa-free destinations vs. visa-on-arrival destinations.

India's Rank in the Henley Passport Index 2026

As of the 2026 Henley Passport Index, India holds approximately rank 85th with a visa-free/visa-on-arrival score of around 60+ destinations. This marks a steady improvement over the past decade, driven by bilateral agreements, growing diplomatic influence, and India's economic rise.

How a Schengen Visa Helps Indian Travelers

Indian passport holders cannot visit most European countries visa-free. However, obtaining a Schengen visa effectively adds 29 European countries to your accessible list. With a single Schengen visa, you can explore France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Switzerland, Netherlands, and more — all on one trip.

Moreover, having a Schengen visa on your passport improves your travel profile. Many countries (like Turkey, Mexico, and some Caribbean nations) offer simplified visa processes or waiver programs for Indian travellers who hold a valid Schengen, US, or UK visa. How to Improve Your Travel Freedom

Even with an Indian passport, there are proven strategies to improve your access:

1. Build a Strong Travel History

Start by visiting countries that are easier to get into (Southeast Asia, Maldives, Mauritius). A clean travel history with multiple stamps from developed nations makes future visa applications more favourable.

2. Obtain Multi-Entry Visas for Gateway Countries

A multi-entry Schengen visa, US B1/B2 visa, UK visitor visa, or Canadian visa acts as a "passport booster." Many countries offer eVisa or visa-free access specifically to Indian travellers holding these visas.

3. Apply for Stronger Passports Through Citizenship or Residency

Some countries offer citizenship by investment or residency-by-investment programs. While not affordable for everyone, programs in Portugal (Golden Visa), Malta (MERP), or Caribbean nations (St. Kitts, Antigua, Dominica) can provide a second passport with significantly better travel freedom.

4. Strengthen Your Visa Application Profile

Maintain a healthy bank balance, stable employment, clear travel purpose, and comprehensive documentation. A strong visa application profile increases approval chances and can lead to longer-validity visas over time. For Schengen visa tips, read our Schengen Visa Guide.

Key Takeaway

While India's Henley Passport Index rank is modest, your individual travel freedom can be significantly higher through strategic visa planning. A single Schengen visa adds 29 destinations; a US visa opens doors across the Americas and beyond.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often is the Henley Passport Index updated?

The index is updated quarterly (January, April, July, and October). This regular cadence ensures it reflects the latest visa policy changes, new agreements, and geopolitical developments.

How does the Henley Index differ from other passport rankings?

The Henley Passport Index is the most widely cited ranking and uses IATA data as its primary source. Other rankings like the Arton Capital Passport Index use a different methodology and may produce slightly different results. Henley's is generally considered the gold standard.

Can having a strong passport help with visa applications for other countries?

Yes. Holding a valid US, UK, Schengen, or Canadian visa (or a second passport from a strong country) significantly improves your visa approval odds for other destinations. Many countries offer simplified procedures for visa holders of certain countries.
