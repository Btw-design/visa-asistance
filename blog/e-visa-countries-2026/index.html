What Is an E-Visa and How Does It Work?

An e-visa is an electronic travel authorisation that allows eligible foreign nationals to enter a country for tourism, business, transit, or medical purposes. The entire processâ€“from filling out the application form to uploading documents and receiving the approvalâ€“is conducted online. The approved e-visa is linked to your passport number, so you simply present your passport at immigration upon arrival.

How E-Visas Differ from Traditional Visas

Unlike traditional visas, e-visas require no physical paperwork, no embassy visit, and no passport submission. Processing is typically faster (1-5 business days), and you can apply from anywhere with an internet connection. However, e-visas may have shorter validity periods and fewer permitted purposes of travel compared to traditional visas.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fastest e-visa to get?

Turkey offers the fastest e-visa, with instant approvals for most eligible nationalities. Singapore's SG Arrival Card and Malaysia's MDAC are also processed instantly. Among paid e-visas, Sri Lanka (1â€“2 days) and Thailand (1â€“3 days) are among the quickest.

Can I extend my e-visa?

Most e-visas cannot be extended (e.g., India, Turkey, Vietnam). However, some countries like Thailand allow a one-time extension of 30 days at local immigration offices. Always check the specific country's policy before travel.

Is it safe to apply for e-visas online?

Yes, but only when using the official government website of the destination country. Be cautious of third-party agencies that may charge inflated fees or misuse your personal data. Look for ".gov" domains or official embassy links.

Do children need e-visas?

Yes, in most cases, each travellerâ€“including infants and childrenâ€“needs a separate e-visa application. Some countries offer family application options, but individual approvals are still issued per passport.

What documents do I need for an e-visa?

Typically: a valid passport (6+ months validity), a digital passport-style photograph, a scanned copy of your passport bio page, and proof of onward travel (return flight booking). Some countries also require hotel bookings and bank statements.
