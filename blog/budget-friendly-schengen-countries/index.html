5 Budget-Friendly Schengen Countries Beyond Paris and Rome for Indian Travellers

Published: June 10, 2026 9 min read By My Visa Assistance Team Updated: June 10, 2026

When Indian travellers dream of Europe, the first names that come to mind are usually Paris, Rome, London, or Zurich. But here is the truth — these cities are expensive, often crowded, and your money simply does not go as far. A coffee in Paris can cost €5, while the same cup in Budapest costs €1.50. A hotel room in Rome might set you back €120 a night, while Krakow offers charming stays for €30-40.

The Schengen visa opens the door to 29 European countries, many of which are incredibly affordable for Indian travellers. The best part? You pay the same visa fee regardless of which country you choose to visit. So why not pick a destination where your rupee stretches further? Read our Schengen Area guide for the full list of member countries.

Here are 5 budget-friendly Schengen countries that offer unforgettable European experiences without burning a hole in your pocket.

1. Hungary — The Thermal Bath Paradise

Daily Budget: ₹3,500-4,500 | Best for: History, thermal baths, nightlife

Budapest, the stunning capital of Hungary, is arguably the best value city in all of Europe for Indian travellers. The city is split in two by the Danube River — Buda on one side (historic and hilly) and Pest on the other (bustling and modern).

Money-Saving Tip

Visit the Széchenyi Thermal Bath on a weekday morning (opens 6 AM). Entry is €20-25 instead of peak-hour prices. Combine it with a visit to Heroes' Square right next door — completely free!

What to do on a budget:

  • Széchenyi Thermal Baths — Relax in Europe's largest thermal bath complex (~€22)
  • Ruin Bars — Explore abandoned buildings turned into quirky bars in the Jewish Quarter (drinks from €2-3)
  • Fisherman's Bastion — Free panoramic views of the Parliament building across the Danube
  • Great Market Hall — Taste Hungarian goulash and lángos for under €5
  • Margit Island — A free green oasis in the middle of the Danube, perfect for picnics

Getting around: Budapest has one of the cheapest and most efficient public transport systems in Europe. A 72-hour travel card costs just €15 (₹1,350). Taxis are also quite affordable compared to Western Europe.

2. Poland — Rich History on a Shoestring

Daily Budget: ₹3,000-4,000 | Best for: WWII history, affordable luxury, cultural experiences

Poland is hands-down the cheapest Schengen country for Indian travellers. Your daily expenses here can be as low as ₹3,000 if you plan wisely. Krakow and Warsaw both offer incredible historical experiences, excellent food, and surprisingly affordable accommodation.

Money-Saving Tip

Book inter-city buses (FlixBus or PolskiBus) in advance. A ticket from Krakow to Warsaw can cost as little as €5 (₹450) if booked 2-3 weeks ahead — cheaper than a Uber ride in most Indian cities!

What to do on a budget:

  • Auschwitz-Birkenau Memorial — Free entry (book online in advance); guided tours available for ~€10
  • Wawel Castle — Free entry to the grounds; paid tickets for specific exhibitions start at €3
  • Warsaw Old Town — A UNESCO World Heritage site, completely free to explore
  • Wieliczka Salt Mine — A stunning underground world, ~€12 entry
  • Polish dumplings (Pierogi) — A hearty meal for just €3-5

Accommodation: You can find clean, centrally-located hostels for €10-15/night and budget hotels for €25-35/night. Airbnb apartments in Krakow start from as low as €20/night.

3. Czech Republic — Fairy-Tale Towns and Cheap Beer

Daily Budget: ₹3,500-4,500 | Best for: Architecture, beer lovers, romantic getaways

Prague is one of the most beautiful cities in the world, and the Czech Republic is a dream destination for budget travellers. The country is famous for its stunning Gothic and Baroque architecture, charming medieval towns, and — famously — the cheapest beer in Europe (often cheaper than bottled water!).

Money-Saving Tip

In Prague, avoid restaurants on the main tourist streets (Old Town Square and Charles Bridge area). Walk 2-3 minutes into side streets and prices drop by 40-50%. A pint of world-class beer costs just €1.20-1.50 in local pubs.

What to do on a budget:

  • Charles Bridge — Walk this iconic bridge for free at sunrise (avoid the midday crowds)
  • Prague Castle — Free entry to the grounds; the full circuit ticket is ~€12
  • Old Town Square — See the famous Astronomical Clock chime on the hour — free!
  • Český Krumlov — A fairy-tale town a few hours from Prague; entry to the castle is free
  • Beer Spa — Yes, a spa where you bathe in beer! ~€40 for a unique experience

Day trips: Take a day trip to KutnÃ¡ Hora (Sedlec Ossuary — the bone church) for just €4-5 by train. The charming town of Karlovy Vary (a spa town) is also a cheap and scenic getaway.

4. Portugal — Atlantic Beauty on a Budget

Daily Budget: ₹4,000-5,000 | Best for: Coastal scenery, pastries, laid-back culture

Portugal has become increasingly popular among Indian travellers — and for good reason. Lisbon and Porto offer incredible value compared to other Western European capitals. The Atlantic coastline, colourful architecture, and the famous pasteis de nata make Portugal an unforgettable destination.

Money-Saving Tip

Buy a Viva Viagem card in Lisbon (€0.50) and load it with daily passes. A day of unlimited travel on metro, buses, and trams costs just €6.40. In Porto, the Andante card works similarly. The iconic Tram 28 is charming but the regular bus 28 follows the same route at a fraction of the cost!

What to do on a budget:

  • Belém Tower — Admire from outside for free; skip the €12 entry queue
  • Pasteis de Nata — Try the original at Pastéis de Belém (€1.30 each)
  • Alfama District — Get lost in Lisbon's oldest neighbourhood for free
  • Luís I Bridge — Walk across for panoramic views (instead of paying for the cable car)
  • Sintra Day Trip — Train from Lisbon is €2.30 each way; explore the whimsical Pena Palace grounds

Food: A traditional Portuguese meal (grilled sardines, caldo verde soup, pastel de nata) can cost as little as €8-10. The famous "secret" steak sandwich (Bifana) costs around €3.

5. Greece — Ancient Wonders and Island Dreams

Daily Budget: ₹3,500-4,500 | Best for: Ancient ruins, Mediterranean cuisine, off-season island hopping

Greece is often assumed to be expensive, but during the off-season (November-March, excluding Christmas), it is remarkably affordable. Athens is a budget-friendly city filled with ancient wonders, and even the famous Greek islands can be visited cheaply if you avoid peak summer months.

Money-Saving Tip

Visit Greece in the off-season (October-April). Island ferry prices drop by 40-50%, hotels cost half of summer rates, and major archaeological sites are empty. The Acropolis, for example, costs €10 in winter vs €20 in summer.

What to do on a budget:

  • Acropolis — €10 in winter; free on the first Sunday of every month (November-March)
  • Plaka Neighbourhood — Wander through charming white-washed streets for free
  • Mount Lycabettus — Hike up for free sunset views over all of Athens
  • Monastiraki Flea Market — Browse for souvenirs, vintage finds, and cheap street food
  • Off-season Island Hopping — Ferries to islands like Hydra or Aegina cost €10-15

Food: Greek street food is both delicious and cheap. A gyros pita costs €2.50-3.50, souvlaki skewers are €1.50-2, and a Greek salad is €5-7. Tavernas in non-touristy areas serve hearty meals for €8-10.

Money-Saving Tips for Indian Travellers in Schengen Europe

Stretching your rupee in Europe is all about smart planning. Here are our top tips:

  • Cook your own meals — Book accommodation with kitchen access (Airbnb or hostels). A week's groceries cost €20-30 vs €50-70 eating out daily.
  • Use local transport — City cards (like the Budapest Card or Prague Card) offer unlimited transport plus free museum entry. Always do the math first.
  • Free walking tours — Every major European city offers free walking tours (tip-based, €5-10 recommended). They are excellent introductions to the city.
  • City passes — The Budapest Card (€30 for 72 hours) includes free public transport, free entry to 17 museums, and discounts at thermal baths.
  • Travel off-season — November to March (excluding Christmas and New Year) offers 30-50% savings on flights, accommodation, and attractions.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle — Most European cities have safe, free drinking water fountains. Save €1-2 per bottle every day.
  • Book flights in advance — Use flight comparison tools and book 2-3 months ahead. Budget airlines like Ryanair, Wizz Air, and EasyJet connect these countries for as little as €10-20 per leg.

Important: Visa Fee Is the Same Everywhere

The Schengen visa fee is uniform across all 29 member states. Whether you apply through Poland (the cheapest country to visit) or Luxembourg (one of the most expensive), the fee remains the same — approximately ₹8,100 to ₹8,500 (€90) depending on the current exchange rate. So it makes financial sense to choose a budget-friendly destination! Read our complete Schengen Visa Guide for up-to-date fee information and application procedures.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest Schengen country to visit from India?

Poland is generally the cheapest Schengen country for Indian travellers, with a daily budget of just ₹3,000-4,000. Hungary, Czech Republic, and Greece are also very affordable, each ranging from ₹3,500-4,500 per day. Poland offers the best value for accommodation and food.

Is the Schengen visa fee different for different countries?

No. The Schengen visa fee is standardised across all member states. As of 2026, the fee for Indian citizens is €90 (approximately ₹8,100 to ₹8,500 depending on the exchange rate). The fee does not change based on which country you visit. Learn more in our Schengen Visa Guide.

Which is more affordable for Indian tourists — Hungary or Portugal?

Hungary is significantly more affordable than Portugal. Daily expenses in Budapest (₹3,500-4,500) are lower than in Lisbon (₹4,000-5,000). Accommodation, dining, and public transport are all cheaper in Hungary. However, if you prefer coastal scenery and Atlantic culture, Portugal's higher cost may still be worthwhile.

Can I club multiple budget-friendly Schengen countries in one trip?

Absolutely! Poland, Czech Republic, and Hungary are geographically clustered and well-connected by budget buses and trains. A 2-week trip covering Krakow, Prague, Budapest, and Vienna is a popular and affordable itinerary. Just ensure you follow the Schengen Main Destination Rule when deciding which embassy to apply through.

When is the best time to visit these countries on a budget?

The off-season (November to March, excluding Christmas) offers the lowest prices for flights and accommodation. Shoulder seasons — April to May and September to October — provide a perfect balance of pleasant weather and reasonable prices. Summer (June-August) is peak season and significantly more expensive across all five countries.
