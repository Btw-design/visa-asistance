US visa Interview Tips 2026: How to Prepare and Succeed

Published: May 28, 2026 7 min read By My Visa Assistance Team Updated: June 6, 2026

The US visa interview is often the most nerve-wracking step in the visa application process. But with the right preparation, you can walk into that interview with confidence. This guide covers everything you need to know — from document preparation to answering tough questions — to maximize your chances of visa approval. For an overview of different visa types, visit our Visa Guide.

Understanding the US visa Interview

The US visa interview is a mandatory step for most US visa applicants (excluding renewals through the interview waiver program). It is conducted by a consular officer at the US Embassy or Consulate in your home country. The interview typically lasts 2-5 minutes, but those few minutes can determine the outcome of your visa application.

Key Fact

According to the US Department of State, the worldwide B-1/B-2 visa approval rate was approximately 78% in 2025. With proper preparation, you can significantly increase your chances of being in the approved category.

Step 1: Complete the DS-160 Form Correctly

The DS-160 is the online nonimmigrant visa application form. Errors here can lead to rejection, so take your time.

  • Double-check all personal information against your passport
  • Be consistent with your travel history and employment details
  • Upload a compliant photo (2x2 inches, white background, taken within 6 months)
  • Save the confirmation barcode — you will need it for the interview

For detailed document requirements, visit our US visa requirements page or check the Visa Requirements overview.

Step 2: Pay the Visa Fee

The US visa application fee for B-1/B-2 visas is $185 as of 2026. H, L, O, P, Q, R category visas are $205, E category is $315, and K category is $265. You must pay this before scheduling your interview. Keep the receipt — you will need it for your appointment. Check the official US visas website for the latest fees.

Step 3: Schedule Your Interview

Wait times for US visa interviews vary by embassy location. In 2026, average wait times range from 2 weeks to 3 months depending on the country. Schedule as early as possible, especially if you have specific travel dates. Check the official U.S. Embassy wait times for your location.

Common Interview Questions & How to Answer

Here are the most common questions asked during US visa interviews and how to approach them:

QuestionWhat the Officer WantsHow to Answer
What is the purpose of your visit?Clear, legitimate reasonBe specific: "Tourism to visit New York and Washington DC for 10 days."
Have you traveled abroad before?Travel history shows complianceList countries you have visited and whether you returned on time.
How long do you plan to stay?Realistic durationMatch with your itinerary. Typically 1-3 weeks for tourism.
Do you have family in the US?Verify immigration riskBe honest. If yes, explain they are lawful residents/citizens.
What do you do for a living?Strong home country tiesExplain your job, years of service, and career stability.
How will you fund your trip?Financial capabilityShow bank statements and explain how you will cover expenses.

Pro Tips

  • Be concise - Answer directly without unnecessary details
  • Be honest - Never lie or exaggerate. Inconsistencies are red flags
  • Stay calm - The officer is evaluating you, not interrogating you
  • Dress professionally - First impressions matter
  • Speak English - Unless you request an interpreter, the interview is in English

What NOT to Do at the US visa Interview

  • Don't submit fake documents or false information — this leads to permanent ineligibility
  • Don't argue with the consular officer — remain respectful even if denied
  • Don't volunteer unrelated information — answer only what is asked
  • Don't bring electronic devices — phones, smartwatches, and bags are not allowed inside
  • Don't mention plans to work or immigrate on a tourist visa

What Happens After the Interview?

There are three possible outcomes:

  • Approved - Your passport will be collected for visa stamping (typically 3-7 working days).
  • Administrative Processing - Additional checks are needed. This can take 2-8 weeks.
  • Refused - Under Section 214(b) of the Immigration Act. You will receive a refusal letter and can reapply after addressing the concerns.

If your visa is refused, our team at My Visa Assistance can help you understand the reason and strengthen your application for re-submission. Contact us for guidance.

US visa Validity & Entry

For many nationalities, the B-1/B-2 visa is valid for up to 10 years with multiple entries. Each visit allows a stay of up to 6 months, as determined by the Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officer at the port of entry. For most visa categories, you can also check the official UStraveldocs portal for appointment scheduling and application status.
