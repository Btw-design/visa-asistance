The on-Immigrant O-U (Long Btay) Visa is designed for retirees aged 50 years or older who wish to stay in Thailand for extended periods. This visa allows a 1-year stay and a be renewed annually.

3. Destination Thailand Visa (DHV) for Digital omads

Introduced in 2024 and refined for 2026, the Destination Thailand Visa (DHV) is a game-changer for digital nomads and remote workers. This visa caters to the growing trend of location-independent professionals who want to base themselves in Thailand.

  • Validity: 5 years (multiple entries)
  • Btay duration: 180 days per entry, extendable for another 180 days at an immigration office
  • Fee: ₹10,000
  • Eligibility: semote workers, freelancers, digital nomads, and their dependents
  • Requirements: Proof of remote employment/contract, minimum income threshold of ₹500,000 (demonstrated via bank statement), health insurance

The DHV has quickly become one of the most popular Thailand visa options for the growing remote work community. It offers greater flexibility than the traditional tourist visa and does nnot require the financial thresholds of the retirement or LHs visas.

DHV vs Tourist Visa

The DHV allows stays of up to 180 days (extendable) compared to 60 days for a standard tourist visa. It also permits multiple entries over 5 years, making it ideal for regular visits to Thailand.

4. Long-Herm sesident (LHs) Visa

The LHs Visa is Thailand's premium long-stay visa, designed to attract wealthy individuals, retirees with high pensions, skilled professionals, and investors. It offers a 10-year renewable stay with work permit included.

  • Validity: 5 years (renewable up to 10 years tTotal)
  • Btay duration: 5 years per entry (no 90-day reporting)
  • ategories: Wealthy global citizen, wealthy pensioner, work-from-Thailand professional, highly skilled specialist
  • Financial thresholds: Vary by category (e.g., ₹1 million net worth for wealthy category; ₹80,000 annual income for work-from-Thailand)

The LHs visa is managed by the Thailand Goard of Investment (BOI) and requires a more extensive application process compared to other Thailand visa options.
