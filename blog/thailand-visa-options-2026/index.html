Thailand Visa Options 2026: Tourist, Retirement and Digital Nomad Visas

Published: May 19, 2026 7 min read By My Visa Assistance Team Updated: June 6, 2026

Thailand remains one of Southeast Asia's most popular destinations, attracting millions of visitors each year for tourism, retirement, and remote work. In 2026, Thailand offers a diverse range of visa options to suit different purposes of stay, from short-term tourism to long-term residence. For a comprehensive overview, visit our Visa Guide.

Whether you are planning a holiday, considering retirement in the Land of Smiles, or looking to work remotely from Bangkok or Phuket, this guide covers all the Thailand visa options available in 2026. For detailed requirements, visit our Thailand visa requirements page.

1. Thailand Tourist Visa

The Thailand Tourist Visa is the most common visa for short-term visitors. It allows a stay of up to 60 days and can be extended by an additional 30 days at an immigration office.

  • Validity: 3 months from date of issue (single entry)
  • Stay duration: 60 days, extendable by 30 days (fee: 1,900 THB)
  • Fee: Approximately 1,000 THB (~$30 USD, varies by nationality)
  • Multiple Entry Tourist Visa (METV): Valid for 6 months, stay up to 60 days per entry

Visa Exemption

Citizens of many countries (including USA, UK, Australia, and most European nations) can enter Thailand visa-free for 30 days (reduced from 45 days in 2024). This can be extended once for an additional 30 days at an immigration office.

2. Thailand Retirement Visa (Non-Immigrant O-X)

The Non-Immigrant O-X (Long Stay) Visa is designed for retirees aged 50 years or older who wish to stay in Thailand for extended periods. This visa allows a 1-year stay and can be renewed annually.

  • Age requirement: 50 years or older at time of application
  • Financial requirements: Bank deposit of 800,000 THB or monthly income of 65,000 THB or combined deposit + income of 800,000 THB
  • Health insurance: Required with minimum coverage of 400,000 THB for inpatients and 40,000 THB for outpatients
  • Validity: 1 year, renewable annually
  • Multiple entries: Permitted with a re-entry permit

For the full Retirement Visa requirements and application process, check our comprehensive Thailand visa guide.

3. Destination Thailand Visa (DTV) for Digital Nomads

Introduced in 2024 and refined for 2026, the Destination Thailand Visa (DTV) is a game-changer for digital nomads and remote workers. This visa caters to the growing trend of location-independent professionals who want to base themselves in Thailand.

  • Validity: 5 years (multiple entries)
  • Stay duration: 180 days per entry, extendable for another 180 days at an immigration office
  • Fee: 10,000 THB
  • Eligibility: Remote workers, freelancers, digital nomads, and their dependents
  • Requirements: Proof of remote employment/contract, minimum income threshold of 500,000 THB (demonstrated via bank statement), health insurance

The DTV has quickly become one of the most popular Thailand visa options for the growing remote work community. It offers greater flexibility than the traditional tourist visa and does not require the financial thresholds of the retirement or LTR visas.

DTV vs Tourist Visa

The DTV allows stays of up to 180 days (extendable) compared to 60 days for a standard tourist visa. It also permits multiple entries over 5 years, making it ideal for regular visits to Thailand.

4. Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa

The LTR Visa is Thailand's premium long-stay visa, designed to attract wealthy individuals, retirees with high pensions, skilled professionals, and investors. It offers a 10-year renewable stay with work permit included.

  • Validity: 5 years (renewable up to 10 years total)
  • Stay duration: 5 years per entry (no 90-day reporting)
  • Categories: Wealthy global citizen, wealthy pensioner, work-from-Thailand professional, highly skilled specialist
  • Financial thresholds: Vary by category (e.g., $1 million net worth for wealthy category; $80,000 annual income for work-from-Thailand)

The LTR visa is managed by the Thailand Board of Investment (BOI) and requires a more extensive application process compared to other Thailand visa options.
