Vietnam Visa Cost for Indians 2026: Complete Fee Guide for e-Visa, VOA & Tourist Visa

Published: July 7, 2026 12 min read By Ajit Mane Updated 2026

Vietnam continues to be one of the most popular and affordable travel destinations for Indian passport holders. With its stunning landscapes from Ha Long Bay to the rice terraces of Sapa, vibrant cities like Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City, and world-class cuisine, it is no surprise that lakhs of Indian travellers visit Vietnam every year.

One of the first questions every Indian traveller asks is: how much does a Vietnam visa cost? The answer depends on the type of visa you choose, how you apply, and whether you go through an agent. The good news is that Vietnam visa fees are among the most budget-friendly in Southeast Asia, with the standard e-Visa starting at just VND 500,000 (approximately ₹1,615).

This comprehensive guide provides a complete breakdown of Vietnam visa costs for Indians in 2026 — covering every visa type, official government fees, service charges, stamping fees, and hidden costs you should know about.

Vietnam Visa Fee Overview for Indians 2026

Vietnam offers multiple visa options for Indian nationals, each with its own fee structure. The table below provides a quick snapshot of all available visa types and their costs in both Vietnamese Dong (VND) and Indian Rupees (INR). Exchange rates used are approximate (1 VND ≈ ₹0.00323).

Visa Type Code Government Fee (VND) Approx. Cost (INR) Validity Processing Time
e-Visa (Single Entry) EV VND 500,000 ₹1,615 30 days 3–5 working days
Visa on Arrival (Pre-Approval) VOA VND 600,000 ₹1,938 15–30 days 1–2 working days (letter)
Tourist Visa (3 Months) TV VND 1,000,000 ₹3,230 90 days 5–7 working days
Business Visa BV VND 1,200,000 ₹3,876 3–12 months 5–10 working days
Work Visa LD VND 1,500,000 ₹4,845 1–2 years 7–15 working days
Key Insight: For most Indian tourists visiting Vietnam for a short holiday of up to 30 days, the e-Visa at VND 500,000 (₹1,615) is the most cost-effective option. It is completely online, requires no physical submission, and eliminates agent fees.

Detailed Fee Breakdown by Visa Type

1. Vietnam e-Visa (EV) — VND 500,000 (₹1,615)

The Vietnam e-Visa is the most popular choice for Indian travellers. It is issued through the official Vietnam e-Visa portal at evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn. The government fee is a flat VND 500,000, which covers the application processing and digital visa issuance. This fee is non-refundable, even if your application is rejected. There are no additional charges unless you use a third-party service.

The e-Visa allows a single entry for up to 30 days. You must enter Vietnam within 30 days of the visa issuance date. The processing time is typically 3 to 5 working days, though it can extend to 7 days during peak travel seasons.

e-Visa Cost Summary

✓ Government fee: VND 500,000 (₹1,615) — non-refundable
✓ Service fee (if using agent): ₹500 to ₹1,500 extra
✓ Payment method: Online via credit/debit card (additional forex markup of 2–3% may apply)
✓ Total out-of-pocket: ₹1,650 to ₹3,200 depending on agent use and forex charges

2. Vietnam Visa on Arrival (VOA) — VND 600,000 (₹1,938) + Stamping Fee

The Visa on Arrival (VOA) is a two-step process. First, you obtain a pre-approval letter from a licensed Vietnamese travel agency or visa service. This costs approximately VND 600,000 (₹1,938) for the letter processing. Upon arrival at one of Vietnam's international airports (Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, Da Nang, Cam Ranh, or Phu Quoc), you pay a separate stamping fee in cash.

The stamping fee is paid at the visa-on-arrival counter before you clear immigration. It is usually around ₹1,500 to ₹2,000 per person for a single-entry visa. The VOA option is useful if you need a visa quickly (approval letter in 1–2 working days) or if you prefer not to upload documents online.

VOA Cost Component Amount Paid To
Pre-Approval Letter Fee VND 600,000 (₹1,938) Visa agency (online)
Stamping Fee (Single Entry) ₹1,500 – ₹2,000 Immigration counter at airport (cash only)
Stamping Fee (Multiple Entry) ₹2,500 – ₹3,500 Immigration counter at airport (cash only)
Total Estimated Cost ₹3,500 – ₹5,500 —
Important: The VOA stamping fee must be paid in cash (USD or VND) at the airport. Credit cards are not accepted. Carry exact change to avoid delays. The immigration counter typically charges in USD, so having small USD notes is recommended.

3. Tourist Visa (3 Months / Multiple Entry) — VND 1,000,000 (₹3,230)

If you plan to stay in Vietnam for more than 30 days or want the flexibility of multiple entries, the 3-month tourist visa (TV) is your best option. It costs VND 1,000,000 (₹3,230) and allows stays of up to 90 days. This visa is typically processed through the Vietnam Embassy or a visa agency. Processing time ranges from 5 to 7 working days.

The 3-month tourist visa is ideal for digital nomads, long-term backpackers, or travellers who plan to enter and exit Vietnam multiple times (e.g., for side trips to Cambodia, Laos, or Thailand).

4. Business Visa (BV) — VND 1,200,000 (₹3,876)

For Indian professionals travelling to Vietnam for business meetings, conferences, or trade activities, the Business Visa (BV) is required. The government fee is VND 1,200,000 (₹3,876), and the validity ranges from 3 to 12 months depending on the sponsorship from the Vietnamese company.

Business visa applications require a letter of invitation from a registered Vietnamese company along with the company's business license. Processing takes 5 to 10 working days.

5. Work Visa (LD) — VND 1,500,000 (₹4,845)

Indian nationals employed by a Vietnamese company or working on a long-term assignment need a Work Visa (LD). This is the most expensive visa type at VND 1,500,000 (₹4,845), with a validity of 1 to 2 years. The application is a multi-step process involving the Department of Labour, Invalids and Social Affairs (DOLISA) and the Immigration Department.

Work visa costs do not include the work permit fee (which is a separate charge borne by the sponsoring employer). Processing can take 7 to 15 working days or longer.

Money-Saving Tip: For a standard 7–14 day holiday in Vietnam, the e-Visa at ₹1,615 is significantly cheaper than VOA (which costs ₹3,500+ after stamping fees). Apply for your e-Visa at least 2 weeks before travel to avoid rush and the need for an agent.

How to Apply for a Vietnam Visa — Step-by-Step with Costs

1

Choose Your Visa Type

Decide between e-Visa (VND 500,000), VOA (VND 600,000 + stamping fee), or Tourist Visa (VND 1,000,000). For most Indian tourists, the e-Visa is the most affordable and convenient option. Consider your travel duration, number of entries needed, and how far in advance you are applying.

2

Prepare Your Documents

Have your valid Indian passport (minimum 6 months validity from your date of entry), a recent passport-size digital photograph (white background, 4x6 cm), and a scanned copy of your passport bio page. No additional documents are required for a standard e-Visa application.

3

Fill Out the Online Application

Visit the official Vietnam e-Visa portal at evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn. Complete the online form with your personal details, passport information, travel dates, entry and exit points, and accommodation details. Review your information carefully before submission — errors can lead to delays or rejection.

4

Upload Photograph and Passport Copy

Upload your digital passport photo (meeting ICAO standards: 4x6 cm, white background, front-facing, no glasses) and a clear scanned copy of your passport bio page. Poor-quality uploads are a common reason for e-Visa rejections.

5

Pay the Visa Fee Online

Pay the visa fee using a credit or debit card. The government fee is VND 500,000 (₹1,615). Your Indian bank will apply the current exchange rate plus a forex markup of 2–3%. Some Indian credit cards offer zero forex markup — using these can save you ₹30–₹50 on the transaction.

6

Receive Your e-Visa via Email

Once processed (3–5 working days), you will receive your e-Visa as a PDF attachment in your registered email. Print two copies — one to present at immigration checkpoints and one as a backup. Your e-Visa is linked to your passport number, so you do not need to carry additional paperwork.

7

Present e-Visa at Vietnam Immigration

Upon arrival at any Vietnamese international airport, present your printed e-Visa along with your passport at the immigration counter. The immigration officer will verify your details and stamp your passport. The process typically takes 1–2 minutes per traveller.

Hidden Costs Indian Travellers Should Know

Beyond the official visa fees, be aware of these potential hidden costs when planning your Vietnam trip:

Forex Markup on International Payments

Indian banks typically charge 2–3% on international transactions. On a VND 500,000 (₹1,615) payment, this adds ₹32–₹48. Use a zero-forex credit card (like Niyo, Scapia, or IDFC FIRST) to avoid this charge entirely.

Agent Commission for VOA Letters

Some VOA service providers charge hidden commissions. Always compare prices across multiple licensed agencies before purchasing a pre-approval letter. A genuine VOA letter should not cost more than VND 600,000 (₹1,938) plus the stamping fee.

Re-Application Costs on Rejection

All Vietnam visa fees are non-refundable. If your application is rejected due to incorrect information or poor-quality uploads, you must pay the full fee again. Double-check your application before submission to avoid this.

Extension Fees

If you decide to extend your stay beyond 30 days, visa extensions through the Vietnam Immigration Department cost approximately VND 300,000–500,000 (₹970–₹1,615). Extensions are not guaranteed and must be applied for before your current visa expires.

Money-Saving Tips for Indian Travellers

Tip 1: Apply directly on the official e-Visa portal. Avoid third-party websites that charge 2x–3x the actual government fee. The official portal charges exactly VND 500,000 with no hidden fees.
Tip 2: Use a zero-forex card. Cards like Niyo Global, Scapia, or IDFC FIRST Bank credit cards charge no forex markup on international transactions, saving you 2–3% on every payment.
Tip 3: Apply at least 2–3 weeks before travel. Last-minute applications often force travellers to use the more expensive VOA (₹3,500+) or pay urgent processing fees to agents.
Tip 4: Carry USD cash for emergencies. If you opt for VOA, the stamping fee is best paid in crisp USD bills. Avoid exchanging currency at the airport where rates are unfavourable.
Tip 5: Check if you qualify for visa exemptions. Indian diplomatic and official passport holders may be exempt from visa fees for stays up to 90 days. Additionally, Phu Quoc Island offers a visa-free stay of up to 30 days for Indian tourists arriving directly on the island.
Tip 6: Travel with a group. Some visa agencies offer discounted rates for group applications (4+ people). Group discounts can reduce per-person costs by 10–15% for VOA or agent-assisted e-Visas.

Vietnam Visa Fee Update 2026 — What Changed

For 2026, the Vietnam government has maintained the visa fee levels from the previous year. The e-Visa fee remains at VND 500,000 and the VOA pre-approval fee at VND 600,000. The key change in 2026 is the continued availability of 90-day e-Visa options for certain nationalities, though Indian passport holders are currently limited to the 30-day single-entry e-Visa.

The government has also expanded the list of international airports where e-Visa holders can enter and exit Vietnam, now covering all 8 international airports including Phu Quoc, Cat Bi (Hai Phong), and Can Tho.
