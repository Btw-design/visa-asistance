India e-Visa Application Process 2026: Step-by-Step Guide

Published: May 25, 2026 9 min read By My Visa Assistance Team Updated: June 6, 2026

India's e-Visa system has made visiting this incredible country easier than ever. Whether you are planning to see the Taj Mahal, explore Rajasthan's forts, or do business in Mumbai, the India e-Visa allows you to apply online without visiting an embassy. This guide walks you through the entire process step-by-step. For an overview of visa types across different countries, visit our Visa Guide.

What is the India e-Visa?

The India e-Visa is an electronic travel authorization that allows citizens of eligible countries to visit India for tourism, business, medical treatment, or conferences. It was introduced to streamline the visa process and promote tourism. The e-Visa is linked electronically to your passport — no physical visa sticker is required.

Available Airports and Seaports

India e-Visa is valid at 30 international airports (including Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Goa) and 6 major seaports. You must arrive at one of these designated entry points.

Eligibility: Who Can Apply?

Citizens of 169+ countries are eligible for the India e-Visa, including the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, Germany, France, Japan, South Korea, Russia, Brazil, and many more. Check your eligibility on our India Visa Guide page or the official Indian visa website.

Documents Required

You will need the following documents for your India e-Visa application:

  • Valid Passport - Must be valid for at least 6 months from your date of arrival in India
  • Recent Digital Photograph - JPEG format, white background, 2x2 inches or 35x45mm
  • Scanned Passport Page - The bio-data page in PDF format (max 300KB)
  • Valid Email Address - Your approved e-Visa will be sent via email
  • Proof of Onward Travel - Return flight booking or onward journey ticket

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Visit the Official Website - Go to the official Indian e-Visa website. Beware of third-party sites charging extra fees.
  2. Fill Out the Application Form - Complete the online application with accurate personal details, passport information, and travel plans.
  3. Upload Documents - Upload your photograph and scanned passport page in the required formats.
  4. Pay the Fee - Pay the visa fee online using a credit/debit card or other accepted payment methods.
  5. Submit & Receive Application ID - Note your application ID for tracking purposes.
  6. Wait for Processing - Most e-Visas are processed within 3-5 business days. Check your email and the status check page.
  7. Download Your e-Visa - Once approved, download the e-Visa and carry a printed copy when you travel.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Top 5 Mistakes That Lead to Rejection

  1. Incorrect passport details - Even one wrong digit can cause rejection. Double-check everything.
  2. Photo not meeting specifications - White background, proper lighting, no shadows. Follow the official photo guidelines.
  3. Using third-party websites - Only use the official government website to avoid extra fees and potential fraud.
  4. Applying too late - Apply at least 4-7 days before travel. Late applications may not be processed in time.
  5. Incomplete information - Fill all required fields. Missing information leads to processing delays.

Processing Time & Fees

Standard e-Visa processing takes 3-5 business days. However, it is recommended to apply at least 7 days before your intended travel date to account for any delays. The e-Visa fee ranges from $25 to $80 depending on the type and duration, with a small processing fee of approximately $2-3.

For a detailed breakdown of fees and requirements, visit our India visa requirements page or check the Visa Requirements overview.

Arriving in India with an e-Visa

When you arrive in India, proceed to the e-Visa counter at immigration. You will need to present:

  • Your printed e-Visa approval letter
  • Passport used for the application
  • Return/onward flight ticket (may be requested)
  • Proof of sufficient funds (rarely requested but good to have)

Immigration officers at major airports like Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore are familiar with the e-Visa system, and the process typically takes 15-30 minutes.

Can You Extend an India e-Visa?

No, India e-Visas cannot be extended or converted into another visa type. If you want to stay longer, you must leave India and apply for a new e-Visa or a regular visa from your home country. Overstaying your visa can result in fines and future travel restrictions.
