Japan Tourist Visa 2026: Complete Guide to Process, Fees, Requirements & Application Tips

Published: July 3, 2026 18 min read By My Visa Assistance Team Updated 2026

Japan — the Land of the Rising Sun — is one of the world's most captivating travel destinations. From the neon-lit streets of Tokyo's Shibuya district and the serene temples of Kyoto to the majestic Mount Fuji and the historic castles of Osaka, Japan offers an unparalleled blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge modernity. For Indian travellers and citizens of many other nations, visiting Japan requires careful visa planning.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about the Japan Tourist Visa (Temporary Visitor Visa) in 2026 — who needs a visa, the step-by-step application process, required documents, fees, processing times, and expert tips to ensure a successful application for your dream Japan trip.

Quick Summary: Japan Tourist Visa at a Glance

Visa Type: Temporary Visitor Visa (Single/Double/Multiple Entry)
Fee: ¥3,000 (approx. ₹1,100) for single entry; ¥6,000 (approx. ₹2,200) for double/multiple
Processing Time: 5–7 working days (standard)
Validity: Typically 90 days from issue; stay up to 15–90 days per visit
Where to Apply: Japanese Embassy/Consulate or VFS Global Japan Visa Application Centre
Application Window: Up to 3 months before your intended travel date

Types of Japan Tourist Visas

Japan issues the Temporary Visitor Visa for tourism purposes. This visa category covers sightseeing, visiting friends and family, attending conferences (without payment), and short-term recreational travel. The main types are:

Visa Type Entries Stay Duration Validity Fee
Single Entry Tourist Visa 1 entry 15 / 30 / 90 days 3 months from issue ¥3,000 (₹1,100)
Double Entry Tourist Visa 2 entries 15 / 30 / 90 days per visit 6 months from issue ¥6,000 (₹2,200)
Multiple Entry Tourist Visa Multiple entries 15 / 30 / 90 days per visit 1–3 years (case-by-case) ¥6,000 (₹2,200)

Multiple Entry Visa for Indian Citizens

Since 2023, Japan has relaxed its multiple-entry visa requirements for Indian nationals. If you have a sufficient travel history (e.g., previous visits to Japan or other developed countries like the US, UK, Schengen, Australia, or Canada) and strong financial standing, you may qualify for a 1–3 year multiple-entry visa. This is decided on a case-by-case basis by the Embassy.

Japan Tourist Visa Requirements for Indian Citizens

Indian citizens must apply for a Japan Tourist Visa through the Japanese Embassy/Consulate in their jurisdiction or through the authorised VFS Global Japan Visa Application Centre. Below is the complete list of required documents. For a general checklist that applies across all visa types, see our Essential Visa Document Checklist.

Core Documents

  • Valid Passport: Original passport with at least 6 months' validity beyond your intended departure date from Japan. Must have at least 2 blank pages for visa stamping. Include copies of all previous visas and the bio-data page.
  • Japan Visa Application Form: Duly filled and signed. The form is available for download from the MOFA website or the Embassy/Consulate website. Fill it in capital letters (block letters) in black or blue ink.
  • Passport-Size Photographs: Two recent colour photographs (4.5 cm × 4.5 cm or 2” × 2”), white background, taken within the last 6 months. The photo must show a front-facing view with a neutral expression.
  • Cover Letter: A typed letter addressed to the Visa Officer explaining the purpose of your visit, travel itinerary, places you plan to visit, duration of stay, and your commitment to return to India. Include your name, passport number, travel dates, and contact details.
  • Detailed Travel Itinerary: A day-wise schedule of your planned activities in Japan, including dates, cities, attractions, and accommodation details.
  • Flight Reservation / Confirmed Tickets: Round-trip flight itinerary showing entry and exit from Japan. Many travel agents provide a block booking for visa purposes.
  • Hotel Reservations: Confirmed accommodation bookings for the entire duration of your stay in Japan (e.g., hotels, hostels, Airbnbs). If staying with friends/family, a Letter of Invitation and their residence proof may be required.

Financial Documents

  • Bank Statements: Original bank statements for the last 6 months (on bank letterhead or with bank seal/stamp). A minimum balance of ₹2–3 lakh is generally recommended, though there is no officially published minimum.
  • Income Tax Returns (ITR): ITR acknowledgements or Form 16 for the last 3 assessment years. This demonstrates your financial stability and ties to India.
  • Salary Slips: Last 3–6 months' salary slips (if employed).
  • Fixed Deposit / Savings Certificates: Additional proof of financial assets to strengthen your application (FDs, PPF, mutual funds, property documents, etc.).

Employment / Student Documents

  • For Salaried Employees: Employment letter stating your designation, salary, and duration of employment; Leave Approval Letter (NOC) from your employer granting leave for the travel dates.
  • For Self-Employed / Business Owners: Business registration certificate, GST registration, Income Tax Returns for 3 years, and a letter explaining the business.
  • For Students: Identity card from your educational institution, Bonafide Certificate / NOC from the college/university, and a copy of your parents' ITR and bank statements (if financially dependent).
  • For Retired Persons: Pension statements, proof of retirement, and bank statements showing regular pension deposits.
  • For Homemakers / Dependents: Affidavit of financial support from the sponsor, along with the sponsor's bank statements, ITR, and a letter of relationship.

Additional Documents

  • Travel Insurance: While not mandatory for a single-entry visa, travel insurance covering medical expenses (minimum USD 50,000) is highly recommended and may be requested, especially during certain seasons.
  • Previous Passport: If you have held a previous passport, submit a copy of its bio-data page and any previous visas.
  • Proof of Accommodation: If invited by a host in Japan, submit a Letter of Invitation along with their residence certificate (住民票/Juminhyo) and passport copy.
Important: All documents not originally in English or Japanese must be accompanied by a certified translation. Incomplete documentation is the most common reason for visa delays and rejections. Submit original documents where specified, along with photocopies.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Follow these steps to apply for your Japan Tourist Visa:

1

Determine Your Visa Requirement & Type

Check if your nationality requires a Japan visa. If yes, decide whether you need a Single, Double, or Multiple Entry visa based on your travel plans. Review the stay duration you need (15, 30, or 90 days).

2

Identify Your Jurisdiction

Determine which Japanese Embassy or Consulate handles visa applications for your region. India has Japanese Embassies/Consulates in New Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Bengaluru, and Ahmedabad. You must apply at the mission covering your state of residence. VFS Global centres operate under these missions.

3

Download and Complete the Visa Application Form

Download the official Japan Visa Application Form from the Embassy website or VFS Global portal. Fill it in capital letters. Ensure all sections are completed accurately — personal details, passport information, travel history, employment, accommodation, and declarations.

4

Gather All Required Documents

Collect all documents as per the checklist above. Ensure your passport has sufficient validity, your photos meet the 4.5 cm × 4.5 cm specification (white background), and your bank statements clearly show sufficient funds and regular transactions.

5

Book a Visa Appointment

Visit the VFS Global Japan website or the Embassy's appointment portal to book a slot. Appointments are typically available 2–4 weeks in advance. During peak travel seasons (March–May for cherry blossoms, October–November for autumn colours), book well ahead.

6

Attend the Appointment & Submit Documents

Visit the VFS Global Centre or Embassy on your scheduled date and time. Submit the original and photocopied documents, provide biometrics (photograph and fingerprints) if required, and pay the visa fee. For VFS Global, a service fee applies in addition to the visa fee.

7

Track Your Application

After submission, you will receive a tracking ID to monitor the status of your application online. You may also receive SMS updates if you opted for that service. Standard processing takes 5–7 working days.

8

Collect Your Passport

Once processed, you can collect your passport from the centre or have it couriered to your address. If approved, verify the visa sticker details immediately — name spelling, passport number, validity dates, and number of entries. Report any discrepancies to the Embassy immediately.

Pro Tip: Apply at least 4 weeks before your planned travel date to allow sufficient processing time. During peak seasons (Golden Week in Japan, Diwali, Christmas holidays), processing may take longer due to higher application volumes. Never book non-refundable flights or hotels until your visa is approved.

Tips for a Successful Japan Visa Application

Submit a Complete and Accurate Application

Double-check every field in the application form. Ensure names match your passport exactly, dates are correct, and no fields are left blank (write "N/A" where not applicable). Inconsistencies are a top reason for rejections.

Demonstrate Strong Financial Standing

Show a healthy bank balance (₹2–3 lakh minimum), regular income through salary slips, and long-term financial stability through ITR filings. Avoid large, unexplained deposits before applying.

Show Strong Ties to Your Home Country

Japan visa officers need assurance that you will return after your trip. Submit proof of stable employment, family relationships, property ownership, or business interests in India.

Provide a Realistic Itinerary

Your day-wise itinerary should be plausible and well-researched. Don't over-schedule activities or claim to cover too many cities in a short period. Match your hotel bookings with the itinerary dates.

Include Travel History

If you have travelled to other countries (especially Japan, US, UK, Schengen, Australia, Canada), include copies of those visas and entry/exit stamps. A good travel history significantly strengthens your application.

Apply Well in Advance

Submit your application at least 3–4 weeks before your planned departure. Avoid last-minute applications, as they can arouse suspicion and may not leave enough time if additional documents are requested.

Common Reasons for Japan Visa Rejection

Understanding why visas get rejected can help you avoid the same pitfalls. For a comprehensive document preparation guide, see our Essential Visa Document Checklist:

  • Incomplete or Incorrect Documentation: Missing documents, unsigned forms, or incorrectly filled applications.
  • Insufficient Funds: Bank balance too low to support the trip, irregular account activity, or unexplained large deposits.
  • Weak Ties to Home Country: Single, young applicants with no stable employment, property, or family commitments may be seen as higher risk.
  • Poor Travel History: First-time travellers or those with a history of overstaying visas in other countries.
  • Unclear or Unrealistic Itinerary: Vague travel plans, conflicting bookings, or an itinerary that doesn't make geographical sense.
  • False Information: Providing forged documents or false information leads to automatic rejection and potential future bans.
  • Previous Immigration Violations: Overstays or visa violations in Japan or any other country.
What to do if rejected? If your Japan visa application is rejected, the Embassy does not provide a detailed reason. You can re-apply after addressing potential gaps — strengthen your financial documents, improve your itinerary, or apply after building a better travel history. A rejection does not permanently bar you, but multiple rejections without improvement may make future approvals harder.

After Your Visa is Approved

Pre-Departure Checklist

  • Verify Visa Details: Check that your name, passport number, validity dates, and number of entries are correct on the visa sticker.
  • Travel Insurance: Purchase comprehensive travel insurance covering medical emergencies, trip cancellation, lost baggage, and COVID-19-related issues.
  • Carry All Documents: Keep a physical and digital copy of your visa, passport, flight tickets, hotel bookings, itinerary, and insurance certificate.
  • Register Online: Japan has an online immigration pre-arrival system. Complete the Visit Japan Web registration (visit-japan-web.digital.go.jp) for faster immigration clearance.
  • Currency & Cards: Carry some Japanese Yen (¥) in cash for small purchases and taxis. Major credit/debit cards are widely accepted in cities. Suica or PASMO IC cards are essential for public transport.
  • SIM Card / eSIM: Get a Japan-specific travel SIM or eSIM for mobile connectivity. Options include Mobal, Sakura Mobile, Ubigi, and Airalo.

Arrival Procedure

Upon arrival at a Japanese airport, proceed to the immigration counter with your passport, visa (if applicable), and completed Visit Japan Web QR code. The immigration officer may ask about the purpose of your visit, length of stay, accommodation, and funds. Answer honestly and confidently. After immigration, collect your baggage and proceed through customs (declare any dutiable items).

During Your Stay

  • Always carry your passport (or a copy) with you — it's a legal requirement in Japan.
  • Follow local laws and customs: remove shoes before entering homes, don't eat while walking, dispose of garbage correctly, and observe quietness on public transport.
  • Do not overstay your visa. If you need to extend, visit the nearest Regional Immigration Bureau before your current permission expires.
  • The temporary visitor status strictly prohibits paid employment or business activities in Japan.

Japan is a popular destination for Indian travellers, much like Singapore and Thailand. Here are some essential tips to make your first trip to Japan smooth and enjoyable:

Japan Travel Tips for First-Time Visitors

Get a Japan Rail Pass

If you plan to travel between cities (e.g., Tokyo → Kyoto → Osaka), the Japan Rail Pass (JR Pass) offers excellent value. Purchase it before arriving in Japan (it's not sold domestically). A 7-day pass costs about ¥50,000 and pays for itself if you do even one round trip between Tokyo and Kyoto.

Stay Connected with Pocket WiFi

Rent a pocket WiFi device from companies like Japan Wireless, Ninja WiFi, or Global Advanced Communications. Pick it up at the airport and return before departure. Alternatively, purchase a Japanese SIM card or eSIM for your phone.

Japan is Still a Cash-Based Society

While card usage is growing, many small shops, restaurants, temples, and local markets only accept cash. Carry sufficient yen, especially when visiting rural areas or smaller establishments. Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson) have ATMs that accept international cards.

Use Translation Apps

While English signage is available at major train stations and tourist attractions, having a translation app like Google Translate (with Japanese offline pack downloaded) will be immensely helpful in restaurants, local trains, and non-touristy areas.

Best Times to Visit

Spring (March–May): Cherry blossom season — stunning but crowded and expensive.
Autumn (October–November): Beautiful autumn foliage, mild weather.
Summer (June–August): Hot and humid, with many festivals (matsuri) and fireworks shows.
Winter (December–February): Cold but offers winter sports, hot springs (onsen), and fewer crowds.

Follow Japanese Etiquette

Bowing is the customary greeting, remove shoes when entering homes/ryokans, don't tip (it's not practiced), don't eat while walking, and be quiet on public transport. Japanese culture values politeness, punctuality, and respect for others.
