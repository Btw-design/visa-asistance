What is the V-39 Form?

The V-39 (formerly Form 14A Appendix) is the Local Contact/Sponsor Form required for business visa applicants. The Singapore host company must fill and submit this form as part of your visa application. It serves as an undertaking by the Singapore company for your visit and confirms the business purpose of your trip.

Application Process Overview

1

Prepare Documents

Gather all required documents as per the checklist above. Ensure colour scan copies are clear and all details are visible.

2

Submit Application

Submit your visa application through the authorised visa centre (e.g., VFS Global Singapore Visa Application Centre) or the nearest Singapore High Commission / Embassy.

3

Pay Visa Fee

Pay the applicable visa fee and service charges at the visa application centre. Keep the receipt for reference.

4

Biometrics & Photo

Visit the visa application centre in person to submit biometrics (fingerprints) and your photograph.

5

Track Application

Track your visa application status online using the reference number provided at submission. Processing typically takes 3-8 working days.

6

Collect Passport

Once a decision is made, collect your passport from the visa application centre or opt for courier delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to process a Singapore visa?

Standard processing takes 3-8 working days. However, it can vary depending on the embassy workload and your application profile.

Can I apply for a Singapore visa online?

Visa applications for Indian citizens are typically submitted through authorised visa centres like VFS Global or directly at the Singapore High Commission. Some applications can be lodged through ICA's SAVE e-Service if you have a Singapore local contact with a SingPass ID.

What is the visa fee for a Singapore visa?

The visa fee is approximately SGD 30 (around â‚¹1,800-2,000), plus applicable service charges at the visa application centre. Fees are subject to change.

Can I extend my Singapore visa?

Visa extension is not typically granted for tourist or business visit visas. You must depart before your authorised stay expires. Overstaying is a serious offence and can result in fines, deportation, or a ban from re-entering Singapore.

Is a hotel booking necessary for a business visa?

If the Singapore host company is arranging accommodation, hotel booking is not required. However, if you are arranging your own stay, hotel booking is recommended as proof of accommodation.

Do I need travel insurance for a Singapore visa?

While not mandatory for Indian passport holders, travel insurance is highly recommended to cover medical emergencies, trip cancellations, and baggage loss during your stay in Singapore.
