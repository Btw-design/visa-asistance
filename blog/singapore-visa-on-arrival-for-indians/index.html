Singapore Visa on Arrival for Indians 2026: VFTF, eVisa & Complete Guide

Published: July 9, 2026 14 min read By My Visa Assistance Team Updated 2026

Singapore remains one of the most popular travel destinations for Indian travellers, whether for tourism, business, or as a transit hub for onward journeys to Southeast Asia, Australia, or the Americas. One of the most frequently asked questions by Indian passport holders is whether Singapore offers a visa on arrival facility for Indians.

The short answer: Singapore does not offer a standard tourist visa on arrival for Indian passport holders. However, there is a valuable alternative — the Visa-Free Transit Facility (VFTF) — which allows eligible Indian travellers to stay in Singapore for up to 96 hours without a visa when transiting to a third country. For those who need a longer stay or do not qualify for VFTF, a regular visa application through an authorised agent or local sponsor is required.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about entering Singapore as an Indian national in 2026 — from the VFTF transit rules to the full eVisa application process, required documents, fees, processing times, and expert tips for a hassle-free experience.

Understanding Singapore's Visa Policy for Indians

India is on Singapore's list of visa-required countries. This means Indian passport holders generally need to obtain a valid visa before travelling to Singapore for tourism, business, or social visits. However, there are specific scenarios where a visa may not be required — the most important being the Visa-Free Transit Facility.

Visa-Free Transit Facility (VFTF) — The Closest Thing to Visa on Arrival

The Visa-Free Transit Facility (VFTF) is Singapore's transit programme that allows Indian passport holders to enter Singapore without a visa for a short stay of up to 96 hours (4 days), provided they are in transit to or from a third country. This is often referred to informally as a "visa on arrival" for Indian travellers, although technically it is a waiver rather than a visa issued at the airport.

VFTF Eligibility Criteria for Indian Passport Holders

To qualify for the VFTF, Indian travellers must meet all of the following conditions:

  • Valid passport with at least 6 months' remaining validity from the date of entry into Singapore.
  • Confirmed onward air or sea ticket to a third country (not the country of origin). You must depart Singapore within 96 hours of arrival.
  • Valid visa or entry facility for the next destination (if required). For example, if you are transiting to Australia, you must hold a valid Australian visa.
  • Immigration clearance — VFTF is granted at the discretion of the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA) officer at the checkpoint. Approval is not guaranteed.
  • Travel by air or sea — You must arrive and depart by air or sea. Land entries from Malaysia are not eligible for VFTF.

VFTF Key Points

✓ The 96-hour period starts from the time of immigration clearance, not flight arrival.
✓ You cannot extend your stay beyond 96 hours under VFTF — no extensions are permitted.
✓ You must leave Singapore within 96 hours; overstaying attracts strict penalties including fines, deportation, and possible bans.
✓ VFTF is a single-entry facility. If you re-enter Singapore later, you will need a visa or a separate VFTF approval (subject to ICA discretion).
✓ You may enter Singapore via Changi Airport, Seletar Airport, or the Singapore Cruise Centre / Marina Bay Cruise Centre.

VFTF — Eligible Travel Scenarios

1

India to Third Country via Singapore (Outward Transit)

You fly from India (e.g., Mumbai) to Singapore, then connect to a flight to a third country (e.g., Australia, Japan, USA) within 96 hours. You may exit the transit area and stay in Singapore for up to 4 days.

2

Third Country to India via Singapore (Return Transit)

You fly from a third country (e.g., Thailand) to Singapore, then connect to a flight to India within 96 hours. VFTF applies on return transit as well.

3

Third Country to Another Third Country via Singapore

You are travelling from one country (e.g., UK) to another country (e.g., Australia) with a stopover in Singapore of up to 96 hours. This qualifies for VFTF as long as both your arrival and departure are by air or sea.

Important: VFTF does NOT apply if your journey is from India to Singapore and back to India (round trip without a third country). In this case, you must apply for a regular visa before travelling. Also, VFTF is not available for travellers arriving from Malaysia by land (via the Johor-Singapore Causeway or Second Link).

VFTF Eligible Countries for Indian Passport Holders

The VFTF is available to Indian nationals who hold a valid visa or residence permit from any of the following countries. If you have a valid visa from one of these nations, you can transit visa-free through Singapore for up to 96 hours:

  • United States of America (USA) — any valid visa
  • United Kingdom — any valid visa
  • Australia — Permanent Resident or valid visa
  • Canada — Permanent Resident or valid visa
  • Japan — Permanent Resident or valid visa
  • Germany — Permanent Resident or valid visa
  • New Zealand — Permanent Resident or valid visa
  • Switzerland — Permanent Resident or valid visa
  • Republic of Korea (South Korea) — Permanent Resident or valid visa
  • European Union / Schengen Area — any valid visa or residence permit
    • Pro Tip: Even if you hold a valid visa from one of these countries, the final decision to grant VFTF rests with the ICA officer at the checkpoint. Always carry printed copies of your valid visa, onward tickets, hotel bookings, and sufficient funds proof to present if asked. Immigration officers have full discretion to deny entry even if you meet all VFTF criteria.

    Regular Singapore Visa for Indians

    If you do not qualify for VFTF — for example, if you are visiting Singapore solely for tourism without an onward ticket to a third country, or if you plan to stay longer than 96 hours — you must obtain a visa before you travel.

    Application Routes

    There are three main ways to apply for a Singapore visa as an Indian national:

    Application Route How It Works Processing Time Fee
    SAVE System (Local Sponsor) A Singapore citizen or Permanent Resident (PR) aged 21+ with a SingPass ID submits the application online via the ICA e-Service portal. 3 to 5 working days SGD 30 (paid by sponsor)
    Authorised Visa Agent You engage a registered visa agent in India or Singapore who submits the application on your behalf through the SAVE system. 5 to 8 working days SGD 30 visa fee + agent service fee (varies)
    Singapore Embassy / High Commission You submit a paper application in person or by mail to the nearest Singapore diplomatic mission in India. 5 to 10 working days Visa fee varies by mission (approx. INR 1,500 to 2,500)

    Step-by-Step Application Process

    1

    Determine Your Eligibility and Route

    Decide whether you qualify for VFTF (96-hour transit) or need a full visa. If you need a visa, choose your application route: local sponsor, authorised agent, or embassy.

    2

    Complete Form 14A

    Download the official Singapore visa application form (Form 14A) from the ICA website. Fill it out accurately with your personal details, passport information, travel itinerary, accommodation, employment details, and declarations. Sign and date the form.

    3

    Gather Supporting Documents

    Collect all required documents as per the checklist below. Ensure your passport has at least 6 months validity, your photographs meet specifications (35mm x 45mm, white background), and your financial documents are up to date.

    4

    Submit Your Application

    Submit your completed Form 14A and supporting documents through your chosen route. For SAVE, your sponsor uploads everything online. For agents or embassies, submit physical or scanned copies as required.

    5

    Pay the Visa Fee

    The non-refundable visa processing fee is SGD 30 (approximately INR 1,850). This is paid at the time of submission. Authorised agents may charge an additional service fee ranging from INR 1,000 to 3,000.

    6

    Wait for Processing

    Standard processing takes 3 to 8 working days depending on the application route. Check your application status through the ICA e-Service portal or your agent. Avoid making non-refundable travel bookings until your visa is approved.

    7

    Receive Your e-Visa

    Once approved, your visa is issued as a digital e-Visa sent by email. Carry a printed copy when travelling. Upon arrival in Singapore, you will receive an electronic Visit Pass (e-Pass) by email.

    Visa Validity and Stay Duration

    It is important to understand the difference between visa validity and permitted stay duration:

    • Visa Validity: The period during which you may use the visa to enter Singapore. Typically, tourist visas are issued with a validity of 5 weeks (63 days) from the date of issue, but this can vary at the discretion of ICA.
    • Permitted Stay (Visit Pass): The number of days you are allowed to remain in Singapore upon each entry. This is determined by the immigration officer at the checkpoint and is usually up to 30 days for Indian nationals with a valid visa.
    • Multiple Entries: Most tourist visas issued to Indian nationals are single-entry. However, you may request a double-entry or multiple-entry visa in your application, though approval is at ICA's discretion.
    Important: Do not confuse visa validity with permitted stay. Even if your visa is valid for 9 weeks, you may only stay for 30 days per entry. Overstaying your Visit Pass is a serious offence under Singapore law and can result in fines, deportation, and a ban from re-entering Singapore.

    Common Reasons for Visa Rejection

    Understanding why Singapore visa applications are rejected can help you prepare a stronger application. Here are the most common reasons:

    • Incomplete or incorrect documentation — Missing documents, incorrectly filled forms, or discrepancies in information are the leading causes of rejection.
    • Insufficient financial proof — Bank statements that do not show adequate funds or regular income. Ensure your balance reflects at least INR 80,000 to 1,00,000 per person for a short trip.
    • Weak travel history — First-time travellers or those with no prior international travel may face additional scrutiny. Include previous visa copies if available.
    • Unclear travel itinerary — Vague or unrealistic travel plans, contradictory flight and hotel bookings, or missing accommodation details.
    • Previous visa violations — Overstays, deportation, or visa rejections from Singapore or other countries can negatively impact your application.
    • Insufficient ties to home country — The visa officer must be satisfied that you will return to India after your visit. Strong employment, family, and property ties help establish this.
    • Invalid sponsor details — If applying through the SAVE system, your local sponsor must be a genuine Singapore citizen or PR aged 21 or above with a valid SingPass ID.
    Pro Tip: If your visa is rejected, carefully review the reason provided in the rejection letter. Address the specific gaps — whether it is insufficient funds, weak itinerary, or missing documents — before reapplying. Repeated applications without substantive changes are likely to be rejected again. You may also appeal through your authorised agent or sponsor with new supporting documents.

    SG Arrival Card — Mandatory for All Travellers

    Every traveller entering Singapore, whether under VFTF or with a regular visa, must submit the SG Arrival Card (SGAC) online before arrival. This electronic card replaced the paper disembarkation card and includes a health declaration component.

    How to Submit the SG Arrival Card

    • Visit the official ICA e-Service portal or download the MyICA mobile app.
    • Submit the card within 3 days before your arrival in Singapore. Early submissions are not accepted.
    • You will need your passport details, flight information, accommodation address, and a valid email address.
    • The submission is free of charge. You will receive a confirmation email with a QR code — save it on your phone or carry a printout.
    • All family members, including children and infants, must have separate SG Arrival Card submissions.

    Tips for a Smooth Singapore Entry Experience

    Carry Printed Copies of All Documents

    Even though Singapore uses digital systems, always carry printed copies of your e-Visa (if applicable), onward tickets, hotel bookings, bank statements, and SG Arrival Card confirmation. Immigration officers may ask to see physical copies.

    Submit SG Arrival Card Before Your Flight

    Complete the SG Arrival Card online within 3 days of arrival. Do not wait until you reach Singapore. Airlines may check for SGAC submission at check-in, and failure to submit can delay immigration clearance.

    Arrive During Immigration Officer Hours

    While Changi Airport operates 24 hours, having clear travel documents ready and travelling during standard hours can make immigration smoother. Early morning and late evening flights may have fewer officers on duty.

    Know Your Accommodation Details

    Have your hotel name, address, and booking confirmation number ready. If staying with friends or family, have their full name, address, and contact number. ICA officers commonly verify accommodation information.

    Be Honest and Clear with Immigration Officers

    Answer all questions truthfully and directly. State the purpose of your visit clearly (transit, tourism, business meetings). If you are unsure about your travel plans, do not guess — say you will confirm. Inconsistencies can lead to denial of entry.

    Respect Singapore's Laws

    Singapore has strict laws regarding drugs, chewing gum, smoking in public places, littering, and jaywalking. Penalties can include heavy fines or imprisonment. Always carry your passport as identification during your stay.

    Special Scenarios

    Indians with US, UK, or Schengen Visas

    If you hold a valid visa from the United States, United Kingdom, or a Schengen Area country, you may be eligible for the VFTF even without a Singapore visa. This is one of the most common ways Indian travellers visit Singapore for short stopovers. Ensure your visa is valid at the time of transit and you have an onward ticket to a third country.

    Diplomatic and Official Passport Holders

    Holders of diplomatic, official, or service passports may be exempt from Singapore visa requirements. Check with the Singapore Ministry of Foreign Affairs or your nearest Singapore embassy for specific guidance.

    Indians Residing Abroad (NRIs)

    Indian nationals who are permanent residents or long-term residents of countries like the USA, UK, Canada, Australia, or EU nations may still need a visa to enter Singapore unless they qualify for VFTF. NRI status does not grant automatic visa-free entry to Singapore. You must follow the same visa rules as any other Indian passport holder.

    Families and Minors Travelling Together

    Each traveller, including children and infants, requires their own passport and (if applicable) visa application. Minors under 18 must submit additional documents including birth certificates, parental consent letters if travelling with only one parent, and school ID or NOC if applicable.
