Tips for Contacting Embassies

  • Always check the embassy's website for the most up-to-date information before visiting.
  • Most embassies require an appointment for visa services. Walk-in services may not be available.
  • Prepare all required documents before your appointment to avoid delays.
  • Visa processing times and fees are subject to change. Confirm current fees on the embassy website.
  • For urgent visa needs, inquire about expedited processing options when available.
  • If applying from outside the US, contact the nearest embassy or consulate in your region.

Frequently Asked Questions About Embassies

An embassy is the main diplomatic mission located in a country's capital city, headed by an ambassador. A consulate is a smaller diplomatic office usually located in major cities outside the capital, providing limited services like visa processing and citizen assistance. Embassies handle broader diplomatic relations, while consulates focus on specific services.
