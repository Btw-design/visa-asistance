Passport Ranking 2026

Discover the world's most powerful passports ranked by Mobility Score, visa-free access, and global reach.

Top 20 Most Powerful Passports in 2026

Our passport ranking is based on the number of destinations that passport holders can access without a visa, with a visa on arrival, or with an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA). The Mobility Score represents the total number of destinations accessible without a traditional visa application.

The ranking below reflects data from the Henley Passport Index and Passport Index as of June 2026, showing the top 20 most powerful passports for global travel.

How We Rank

Mobility Score = Visa-Free + Visa on Arrival + eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) + eVisa. This represents the total number of destinations a passport holder can visit without obtaining a traditional visa.

World Reach = Percentage of world destinations accessible without a visa. Calculated as (Mobility Score / 195) × 100.

Passport Power Rank = Overall position based on the composite Mobility Score, with ties broken by visa-free count.

Key Insights from the 2026 Passport Ranking

Singapore Leads the World

Singapore claims the top spot with an unmatched Mobility Score of 195, offering visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 97.4% of the world. Its strategic diplomacy and strong international relations continue to expand travel freedom for its citizens.

UAE on the Rise

The United Arab Emirates continues its remarkable ascent, now ranked 9th with 185 destinations. The UAE passport has gained visa-free access to more countries than any other passport over the past decade.

European Passports Dominate

European Union member states dominate the top 20, occupying 18 of the 34 ranked positions. The Schengen Area's collective visa liberalization agreements benefit all EU member states equally.

Passport Power & Economy

There is a strong correlation between a country's economic development and its passport power. Higher-ranked passports enable easier business travel, tourism, and economic exchange, creating a virtuous cycle of global engagement.

Frequently Asked Questions About Passport Rankings

The Mobility Score represents the total number of countries and territories that a passport holder can visit without obtaining a traditional visa. It includes visa-free access, visa on arrival, eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization), and eVisa. A higher score means greater travel freedom.

Singapore holds the strongest passport in 2026 with a Mobility Score of 195, granting access to 159 visa-free destinations, 22 visa-on-arrival destinations, and 9 eTA-eligible territories. Singapore citizens can visit 97.4% of the world without a traditional visa.

The United States passport has seen a relative decline as other countries have expanded their visa-free networks more aggressively. While the US still has strong passport power (ranked 8th with 186 destinations), it has been surpassed by nations like Singapore, Japan, and several European countries that have negotiated broader visa-waiver agreements.

Passport rankings are calculated by counting the number of destinations accessible without a prior visa. The three main categories are: (1) Visa-Free - enter with just a passport, (2) Visa on Arrival - obtain a visa upon arrival, and (3) eTA - Electronic Travel Authorization obtained online before travel. The Mobility Score is the sum of all three categories.

The official Henley Passport Index is available at henleyglobal.com/passport-index. The Passport Index by Arton Capital is available at passportindex.org. Both sources are updated regularly throughout the year as visa policies change.
